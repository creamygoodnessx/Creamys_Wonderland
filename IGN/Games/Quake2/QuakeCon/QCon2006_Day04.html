QuakeCon 2006 - Day Four

> Around 1AM on the final day of the QuakeCon, I was heading out of the ballroom to get a few hours of sleep, but just as I was about to reach the door, I saw a Red Octane metal dance pad on the floor in front of the information desk, and knew right away that someone was setting up Stepmania. I decided that sleep would have to wait a while, since I was looking forward to playing the game at the 'Con for months. As the game was being installed, I gave the guy who was setting up the game my CD of CreamyMix 1, which is my unfinished mix of 11 songs that I had made steps for. This was now added to the other mixes on the machine, including most of the DDR official mixes, as well as two anime theme mixes. Nearly a dozen people including myself took turns playing one song per turn, with the game set to seven songs per game, the fail off, and no time limit on the song selection wheel. The songs I played including included Little Bitch from the official mixes, Havana Club from Bantu Originals, as well as Invader Zim and Breaking the Law from CreamyMix 1. A few hours into the game, play had to be suspended temporarily because a corner of one of the squares was sticking up, but after about half an hour, the problem was fixed and play resumed. At around 6AM, I finally left the ballroom to get what little sleep I still could.

> I woke up around 8:30PM, and after another shower, I gathered all of my possesions from my hotel room except for my dirty laundry, and went to the ballroom to dismantle CES. When I got there, the room had significantly fewer people than it did only a few hours ago, and there were a few large machines scattered about, including forklifts. Once I had CES and all of its paripherals were back in their boxes and checked by security, I wheeled everything back to the front desk in the main building on a wooden dolly I found lying around. I then went back to my room, stuffed my laundry into a plastic garbage bag I got from a cleaning cart in the ballroom, left my room for the last time, and checked out of the Hilton Anatole just after noon. Following my mother's advice, I went to the hotel's buisness center to send the ice maker box and the monitor home via FedEx, so I wouldn't have to lug that weight to the airport and risk losing CES again. I then crammed my laundry bag into my backpack, completely freeing my hands of anything to carry. With nothing left to do at the hotel, I went outside and waited about half an hour for a shuttle bus ride to Dallas Fort Worth. On the bus, I met another QuakeCon attendee named Jitspoe who told me about a few project he had been working on, including a Quake 2 paintball mod called Digital Paint. After getting to Fort Worth, I took the 3:30PM flight back to Phillidelphia.

> Despite not initially having CES with me at the QuakeCon, and not being able to play any games over the network, I still had a really good time at the event. I took over and hundred pictures and shot about 25 movies of the 'Con on my Sony Cybershot, and blew through three pairs of AA batteries and two memory sticks in the process. I also took home two T-shirts, a UV sensitive nVidia plastic cup, and a mousepads as souveniers. During the event, someone would occasionally come by and ask about CES and complement me on it, and those were amongst if not the best of my experience at the event. If I get lucky, I'll be able to go back in two years to the QuakeCon of 2008 with an upgraded CES, as well as a few people to share the experience with.
