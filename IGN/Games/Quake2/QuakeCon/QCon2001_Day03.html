QuakeCon 2001 - Day Three

> On the morning of the 11th, Goatman and I enjoyed breakfast at the local IHOP before heading back to the QCon. We again parted ways, and I headed back to Glamdring, where I played a little Q3A and some Quake2, and even explored other players' machines through the network. That evening, my parents showed up and brought their camera, right on time to take a group shot of several of the Polycounters, myself included. Right after that, my dad took me out to Pizza Hut, and after about a 20 minute wait, returned to QCon with a large pepperoni pie and a large Pepsi. When I walked into the room with all the machines, I noticed about a dozen people gathered around a machine in the back of the room. The computer was connected to a machine called Fullgore, which had a whole bunch of really funny and/or shocking short clips. These clips included people falling off bicycles, failed stunts, fighter plane crashes, bizarre accidents, and some other interesting stuff. A favorite clip amongst the onlookers seemed to be one of a sleeping cat that suddenly woke up and pounced headfirst into a wall. While I was enjoying my pizza and playing Quake, the next to me told me that the guys at id Software were playing against all comers in two-on-two Q3 Team Arena. So I went to the next room where the action was, and noticed that Tim Willits, Todd Hollenshead, Marty Stratton, and some other id guy were sitting together at a row of machines. I got in line and tried to recruit rogue13 as my partner, but he declined, leaving me desperately seeking another partner. Just as I approached the front of the line, I saw Hunter, Mike Jukes, and a few other Polycounters walk into the room. Hunter agreed to be my Quake partner, and we played against Willits and the id guy whose name I can't remember. We lost six captures to zero, and I only got in one frag. Later on in the night, when I was back on Glamdring, I noticed a Quake3 team tournament starting on my table, and on nearby tables. There was this one player who was sitting at my left, who was screaming profanities throughout the tournament. Every time he did, another player would shout 'LANGUAGE!'. I don't know why, but I actually found that rather entertaining. Sometime after midnight, I noticed some people playing Starcraft. I did have some trouble getting on to their network, but after they helped me change a few of my network settings, and after we all upgraded to the latest version of Starcraft: Brood War, we played a four-on-four all Terran game. My team managed to bring down two of the enemy players, but the other two quickly regrouped and got the upper hand. We all ended up allying with each other, drawing the match. I even saved the match on disk, so I could watch it again anytime. I then picked up a few last files from the network before disconnecting and heading back to the Comfort Inn. When Goatman and I returned, it was like 3 in the morning.
