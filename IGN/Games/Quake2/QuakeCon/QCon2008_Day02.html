QuakeCon 2008 - Day Two

> I woke up after six the next morning, and went down to the Business Center to pick up my monitor around 7:30AM, but the door was locked and there was a sign that basically said “be back shortly”. But after a short wait, someone from the inside let me in, and I picked up my monitor and brought it to the BYOC room and finished setting it up around 8AM. About half an hour later, the beeping sound I heard when I initially set up CES was now happening at a much more frantic pace. After inspecting a few wires, I realized that the beeping was coming from the UPS, and that I had plugged the UPS into itself, and CES into the power strip. The monitor had been draining the UPS's power since I plugged it in, and the UPS was not receiving any new power from an outlet, causing its power to drain. So I shut down CES and switched the errant plugs, and this solved the problem.

> With the errant power plug problem out of the way, I still wasn't able to connect to the Internet. I knew that the most likely reason was that I was using the static IP address that I assigned to CES for my home network, and the BYOC used DHCP. However, I didn't know the correct syntax for the ifconfig command to switch to a dynamic IP address, so I went to the nearby help desk for help. Luckily, the guy behind the counter was more knowledgeable about Linux than I was, and fixed the problem by editing a file called /etc/network/interfaces using a command line text editing program called Pico. He commented out the IP settings and set up CES to use a dynamic IP address with the line “iface eth0 inet dhcp”. The next time I ran Firefox, I was able to successfully connect to YouTube.

> Just before 9:30AM, someone recognized me from the QuakeCon two years earlier. It turned out to be Jehar from the TastySpleen community, and he said that his group was sitting in Row B22 in case I wanted to stop by later. I went back to my table, and called my mother to continue the conversation that we started yesterday when I was on the Shuttle Bus. We spent 10 minutes talking about the rest of my experiences at the airport, and the price of the drinks in my hotel room's mini-bar. After hanging up, I went back to my room and brought the six remaining slices of pizza, the 2-liter bottle of Coke, and my purple drink bottle down to CES. Around 10:30AM, I went to the room next to the BYOC room, and watched a few rounds of a Guitar Hero tournament, where two players would compete against each other in a game of GH3 on the Trinity Stage. While in the same room, I got my picture taken with a lady from the Wolfenstein booth, then played Guitar Hero: Aerosmith at another booth, starting off with Walk This Way (Run DMC version) on Easy. I also went to the Bawls booth and bought a can of Bawls, which would normally cost over two dollars a can, for only a dollar.

> Around 12:45PM, some members of the QuakeCon staff brought a box of QuakeCon 2008 T-shirts on the Trinity Stage, and crowd of about a hundred people gathered in front of the stage. The people on the stage tossed the shirts one at a time into the crowd in random places, and the people in the crowd reached for and tried to grab whatever shirts flew in their direction. I tried to catch some shirts that came towards me, but I didn't have any luck. The QuakeCon staff would give away more shirts a few more times throughout the day, but during the times I was there, I either wasn't able to catch any shirts, or took an opportunity to capture the tossings on video.

> Just before 1PM, I met the TastySpleen people at their table. I asked them how I could connect to their server, and they suggested that I use Quake 2's built-in browser to search for a server called BlackBox. I told them I did just that, but the game quit after about a second of each connection attempt. When I said that I was connecting as Agent_Mothman, they said they saw me connect, but couldn't understand why I kept leaving so quickly. The TastySpleen guys said that Blackbox was going to be down for the next hour or so, so I took this time to try to fix whatever error that was preventing me from connecting. I ran Quake 2 from the command line, tried to connect to BlackBox, but got disconnected again with an error that read: "Couldn't write config.cfg". I thought that this might be a permissions related problem, but I found that this wasn't the case after I saw that I owned the permissions to everything in my Quake2 folder. Completely stumped, I decided to give up on Quake 2 for the time being. Not long after that, the person sitting at the machine immediately to my right asked me to make room on my table for his friend who would be arriving sometime later in the event. So I put CES behind my mousepad, and this created about a foot and a half of empty space on the right side of my table.

> Just before 2PM, I went back to the TastySpleen tables, where team member [BTF]DeathStlkr was setting up a server called BTF with infinite ammo. I went back to CES and tried to connect to it, and amazingly, I was able to do so without errors. After I switched to an old fashioned Microsoft ball mouse, the two of us duked it out in a modified version of The Edge for several minutes, and he ended up beating me 15 to 6. The server went back up around 5:30PM, so I joined the server, and once again played on The Edge. At first I was playing against DeathStlkr, and he basically raped my ass six ways to Thursday, scoring nearly 20 frags to my -1 before more people joined in 11 minutes into the match. I started to get some frags in, and ended up in last place with 28 frags when [BTF]DeathStlkr reached the 80 frag limit.

> Sometime around 6PM, I played another round, this time in Tokay's Towers against several players. About fifteen minutes after the round began, an announcement came over the loudspeaker about a case mod contest at the Information Desk. I didn't want to take a chance in missing it, so I cut my Quake 2 game short after scoring 20 frags and went over to the Info Desk to enter. After writing on a list my name, CES's name and row (B14), I was given a red flag with “M3”written on it, and asked to stick it onto my machine. The man behind the desk said that judges would evaluate the machines the next day between 10AM and noon, so I made it a point to try to remember to be at CES at that time. I returned to CES and stuck the flag on the inside edge of the Mario 4” angle iron.

> Sometime after 11PM, I wanted to transfer the pics and movies from my Sony Cybershot digital camera to CES, but I couldn't find the camera's USB cord in either my backpack or CES's box. Thinking that I must have packed the cord in my suitcase, I went up to my hotel room to search for it there, but still couldn't find it. But instead of going back to the BYOC room to check CES's box again, I decided that it would be a good time to go to bed. Besides everything I mentioned above, I also played Stepmania on CES, played more Guitar Hero: Aerosmith, bought a second can of Bawls, and watched some YouTube videos throughout the day.
