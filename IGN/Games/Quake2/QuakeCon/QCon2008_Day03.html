QuakeCon 2008 - Day Three

> After another good night's sleep, I went back to CES in the BYOC room after 8:30AM. The judges for the case mod contest were scheduled to take a look at the machines between 10AM and noon, so I tried my best to be sitting at CES at that time, but I did end up facing a potential distraction. After 11:30AM, more free stuff was being given away at the Trinity Stage. T-shirts were being tossed into an eager crowd, but other things that you wouldn't toss towards people were simply being handed to whoever was at the front of the crowd, such as cans of Bawls, and copies of games. I failed yet again to catch a shirt, but this time I came close. I would also spend this time playing some more Guitar Hero: Aerosmith, but I would always make sure to check CES periodically to see if the judges were there yet. Just before 1PM, the judges for the case mod contest finally came by to evaluate CES. I basically told them that I built CES to look like a game console, which is why I made it small, and with Super Mario Bros. graphics. As soon as they left, some news media people showed up and asked about CES, and I told them basically the same thing I told the judges.

> At this point of my QuakeCon visit, I had been using my Sony Cybershot digital camera a lot, and was afraid that my second pair of AA batteries could run down before end of my vacation. So I went down to the Hilton Anatole's main building and bought a fresh new pair of AA Duracells from a corner shop in the atrium. I was already out of pizza, (I had to throw out the last slice because it got too stale,) so I ordered a small pepperoni pizza with Cinnasticks around 2:30PM, and my order arrived at the hotel less than an hour later. For the next few hours, I would make even more attempts to catch a shirt from the Trinity Stage, but kept failing. One shirt came towards me, but I missed it by inches when I reached for it. It landed right in front of a guy who was sitting on the floor behind me, but he snagged it before I could pick it up. However, just after 6PM that night, my luck changed. I finally caught a shirt, but two other people happened to catch it too at the same time. Luckily, they were nice enought to let me have it, so I finally ended up with an official QuakeCon 2008 T-shirt.

> Soon after catching the shirt, I was passing by the Trinity Stage to check out some of the vendor booths when I saw that free cases of Bawls were being given away on a cart near the stage. I hurried over, and happened to grab one of the last ones on the bottom shelf. This put me in an awkward position, since I now had a case of 24 cans of Bawls, and no way to drink them all before the end of the vacation, and no way to carry them all back to New Jersey. My only option was to take back all the cans I could carry in CES's box, my luggage, and my backpack, and give the rest away. After giving a can to the person sitting to the left of CES, and putting one in my backpack, I carried the rest of the case up to my room, where I put two cans in my suitcase.

> Later on in the night, I found a way I might be able to get rid of the extra Bawls. I decided to give a can to each member of TastySpleen as a way to thank them for making available a Quake 2 server I could play on. I went over to the TastySpleen table asked one of the members (Fanny) if DeathStlkr was still around, and he said that he was watching some tournament finals, but would be back for another round of Quake once the tournament was over. So I went to my room and retrieved six cans of Bawls, and brought them back to my table to hopefully give to the TastySpleen guys once everyone was there. I ended the day by continuing to play on CES through midnight.
