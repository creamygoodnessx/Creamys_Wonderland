QuakeCon 2006 - Day One

> My QuakeCon experience this year had its share of problems, and most of them occurred before I even got to the event. I was supposed to take a flight from Philadelphia that would have gotten me to Dallas Fort Worth sometime after 10AM Texas time, but because I didn't arrive at the airport early enough, I was forced to take the next flight which was scheduled to arrive around 1PM. When I finally got to Dallas, I went to the baggage claim to pick up the ice maker box which contained CES, its UPS, illuminated keyboard, green Razer Copperhead mouse, socks and underwear. But after a few minutes of waiting for the box to appear, the carrousel suddenly stopped, so I thought to myself, "OK, no big deal, the carrousel will start again in a few minutes, I'll pick up my box and be on my merry way". However, after around ten minutes of the machine remaining still with my ice maker box nowhere in sight, I started to panic. It was around then when someone mentioned that we should go to a nearby customer service desk for help, so that's what I did. After a short wait in line, I explained my situation to a lady who had a gimpy right arm, and she gave me an important sheet of paper and told me that the box would be sent to the hotel later that day. I was soon on a ShuttleBus that brought me to the Hilton Anatole where the QuakeCon was being held.

> Just before 3:30PM, I arrived at the hotel and registered for the QuakeCon's BYOC right away. On the way to the registration area, I met Bronco from the Polycount community, and talked to him for a bit. When I got to the registration table, I had planned on registering CES and the rest of my setup, but because most of my stuff was still caught up at the airport, this obviously wasn't an option. However, I was allowed to register my monitor, since I had brought that as a carry-on item on the plane. I was told that I could register everything else once they got to the hotel.

> When I got into the ballroom where the BYOC was being held, I met Bronco again, and this time he was with Frankie, another member of Polycount. After saying hello and snapping this picture of the two, I looked around the BYOC room and saw a lot of custom case mods that some attendees had brought with them. One person had brought a large transparent cube that had red and white lights inside of it, while another case was build from an old vinyl record player with bright purple lights. I picked out a table that was on the left-most row of the room, and setup my BenQ 17" flat screen monitor.

> As I was checking into the hotel at the front desk, I asked the lady behind the counter to be on the lookout for the ice maker box. She said that the front desk would not receive the box, but referred me to the business center, who referred me to another desk, who then referred me to the bell stand. After I explained my situation to them and gave them a description of the box, the people there took down my name, cell phone number, and room number (7187), and said that they would call me when the box arrived. At around 4:30PM, I finally entered my hotel room for the first time. The room had two large beds, a bathroom with a door that didn't lock, and a decent view of downtown Dallas, and because my Uncle Jim and two of my cousins couldn't make the trip with me, I had the room all to myself.

> Sometime that evening, I called the 1-800 number that was on the important sheet the woman at the airport had given me in order to check the status of CES's delivery to the hotel. After going through a fully automated process in which I had to give out the package's file locator number which was printed on the form, I was basically told that the package could not be found. So I called up my mother and explained the call I had just made to the US Airways, and she was just as upset as I was about the situation. She wanted to call the airline herself to see if she could be little more persuasive in getting CES to the Hilton, so I gave her the numbers on the airline's phone number and file locator number. Now that I'd done everything that I could to bring my machine to the event, I decided to return to the BYOC room to take another look around, take some pictures, and hopefully get some free stuff. A little while later, while still in the BYOC room, my mother called me back and said that she had managed to talk to a live person who told her that my box should arrive at the hotel in about an hour. But when that hour had passed, CES still didn't get to the hotel.

> I would talk to my mother at least two more times that evening, and each time she would say that according to US Air, the box was still in Philly. But when I heard from her again around 8PM, she said that the box was in Dallas and would arrive around 9PM. With nothing else to do, I ordered a pepperoni pizza and a 2 liter bottle of Coke from Domino's Pizza and had a couple slices around 8:30PM. This pizza would last me the entire event, and I had to finish it before checking out on the final day, or I would be forced to throw whatever was left of it away. At 9PM, I went to the bell stand and asked the guy there if the box had arrived, but unfortunately it hadn't. However, he also said that the airlines make runs through different hotels, delivering wayward luggage to each of them in turn, and the box should arrive around 1AM. So with nothing to do, I decided to go up to my room and get some sleep, since I probably wouldn't get too many opportunities to do so later on.

> I was awakened by the room's phone around 11PM, and it was the bell stand telling me that the CES's box had finally arrived, so I scrambled to get some clothes on, and then went down to pick it up. The box weighed 40 pounds, and so I wasn't too thrilled about carrying it all the way across the main building, to the building where the BYOC room was, and to CES's future table. So I borrowed a dolly from the bell stand, and used that wheel it to the registration table. Apparently, something I did caught the attention of some cops who were standing at the entrance of the ballroom. They said that my eyes were dilated and that I was acting all hyper, and they seemed to think that I was on drugs or some shit. My keychain had a Swiss Army Knife on it, and in order to keep it from getting taken away, I brought it to my hotel room and returned to registration. I was given a sheet that contained descriptions of CES and its peripherals, and I would need it to bring my things out of the BYOC room. After getting to my table, I set up CES and started up the system just before midnight. CES connected to the network right away without me having to change any IP settings, and I was soon officially part of the BYOC.
