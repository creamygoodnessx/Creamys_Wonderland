QuakeCon 2008 - Day One

> My father dropped me off at Philadelphia International Airport sometime around 9:30AM. On this particular trip, I wanted to avoid another incident with CES's box not arriving at Dallas Fort Worth, so the previous week I had CES and its monitor FedExed straight to the hotel, which was the same Hilton Anatole I stayed at in 2006. This left me carrying only a small suitcase and my backpack. However, I made the mistake of ignoring a piece of advice that my parents and a few other people had been giving me for several years, and it finally caught up to me. I have a keychain that includes a Swiss Army Knife, which is considered contraband on the airlines. On previous flights, I would put the keychain in a purple drink bottle in my luggage case, and I would always manage to sneak it by the airport security nazis. This time around however, my luck ran out, as the TSA fascist pigs pulled me aside, did a thorough search of my bags, found my hidden keychain, and took away my knife, and this held me back half an hour.

> After finding my gate (Gate B16), I did a 15-minute walk to Terminal A to have lunch at McDonalds. My flight was scheduled to leave at 11:35AM, and it was almost 11AM, so I knew I had to hurry back. Soon after reaching Terminal B, I only had to reach Gate B16, but a group of Mastercard vendors pulled me aside to offer me free frequent flier miles. These people were extremely pushy, begging me to hear what they had to say even though I really had to catch my flight, and holding me back another five minutes. To make matters worse, they somehow convinced me to give them my Social Security Number. This pissed me off royally, because I couldn't believe that I was dumb enough to give these pushy motherfuckers my SSN, and this was further compounded by the fact that I had missed my flight, even though I arrived at the gate a few minutes before 11:35. I couldn't help but think that if I had just kept staring straight ahead and just ignored the Mastercard assholes, I could have caught my flight and could have already been in the air.

> I immediately went to the customer service desk for help, and the woman behind the counter gave me a new ticket for a 2:20PM flight to Dallas which would board at Gate C25. I spotted more Mastercard assholes as I was walking through Terminal C, but this time I had the good sense to stare straight ahead and totally ignore them when the called to me. But I was still pissed off about giving away my Social Security Number, that I turned off my phone when my parents tried to call me on my cell phone. The last thing I needed was my parents giving me the whole "I told you so" lecture, and I didn't want to let them know that I did something so goddamn fucking stupid as to give my SSN to people I didn't trust, so I kept my phone off, and planned to not turn it back on until I had arrived in Dallas.

> I boarded my flight around 2PM, and arrived at Dallas Fort Worth after 4:30PM Texas time, then turned my phone back on as I had planned. After a much needed bathroom break, I went to Baggage Claim to call for a Shuttle Bus to the Hilton Anatole. I got on the bus around 5PM, and soon after I did my mother called me on my cell phone and asked me why I hadn't been picking up my phone. I told her about me losing my Swiss Army Knife to the airport nazis, and she gave me the expected "I told you so" response. I was going to tell her about the Mastercard dickheads and my missed initial flight, but she didn't want to talk while I was in a bus with other people, so we agreed to finish the conversation once I got to the hotel.

> After arriving at the hotel around 6PM, the first thing I wanted to do was pick up CES and register it for the QuakeCon's BYOC. So I went to the Bell Stand and asked the man behind the podium where I could find a package if I had one FedExed to the hotel. He directed me to the Business Center, where I signed some sheet of paper and picked up CES's box. But as soon as I did, it occured to me that maybe I should have checked into my hotel room first so that I could put away my suitcase and not have to carry that much more weight around. Fortunately, the woman at the Business Center was nice enough to watch my box as I went to the front desk to check in, but the desk clerk said that even though the room was reserved, it was not actually paid for. I called up my mother and handed the phone to the clerk, and after their conversation, the clerk said that my mother would send her some fax, and that this would resolve the payment issue. I figured that this would be a good time to register for the BYOC, and hopefully the room would be paid for by the time I got back to pick up my room key.

> The BYOC was being held in the same place it was in 2006; in Trinity Hall, which was a building next to the hotel's main building. It would be a long walk, so went back to the Bell Stand and asked for a dolly to carry CES's box. A bellhop showed up with such a dolly, and after I led him to the Business Center to pick up the box, we went to Trinity Hall with CES's box and my suitcase on the dolly, and I got in line for the registration. There, I noticed that a crude arcade had been set up in the room, and included an air hockey table and some classic 8-bit arcade machines. It took nearly an hour to reach the front of the line, where I registered CES and all of my equipment. The man behind the desk, who went by the handle neon, read through a check list that was based on my online registration and listed all the equipment I planned to bring. When he got to the monitor, however, I realized that I had left it in the Business Center. neon told me that because the monitor was on the check list, and I had already been through the registration process, I could just bring the monitor in without having to register again. After being given a blank name tag that I immediately wrote my name on, I went to another table where I was handed a sheet of paper called the QuakeCon 2008 Registration Sheet. This paper would prove that I was the rightful owner of my equipment, and I would need it to take my things out of the BYOC room once the I was ready to go home.

> Once inside the BYOC room with CES, its equipment, and my luggage, I was about to drag all 50+ pounds of my things through the room to find a good table. However, someone at the BYOC front desk offered to watch my things so I could carry CES and its parts one box at a time. I wanted to set up near the main aisle in order to increase the chances of CES appearing in a YouTube video about the event, but couldn't find an end table. The table I eventually did find was potentially better than that, since it was right in front of the Strogg NOC (Network Operations Center), one of two main stages, in Row B14. I set everything up on that table minus the monitor, and began to notice a strange noise that was coming from either CES or the UPS. I heard two beeps at regular intervals, but I thought nothing of it at the time.

> After setting up, I returned to the hotel's front desk at around 8:30PM, and learned that my room had since been paid for. The desk clerk gave me a pamphlet-sized folder that contained two copies of my room's keycard, and told me that my room would be number 4171 in the tower. When I got to my room, I was impressed by it's luxurious nature. It had a full sized couch, a desk that a business executive would own, a king-sized bed, and a really large flat screen TV. I put one keycard in my wallet, and the other in my backpack to ensure that I would not lock myself out. I took the opportunity to take a shower, and I left my room around 10PM to pick up my monitor. Unfortunately, the Business Center had already closed at 9PM, and according to a sign on the door, it would open again at 6:30 the next morning. I decided to pass the time away by getting something to eat, then taking a rest in my room's king-sized bed.

> I went to the hotel's front desk to ask what pizza places in town delivered to the Hilton Anatole. The desk clerk wrote down three Pizza places and their phone numbers; Domino's, Papa John's, and Zini's Pizzeria. Starting at the top of the list, I called Domino's, and they put me on hold for nearly five minutes. I was only seconds away from hanging up and moving on to Papa John's when someone answered. I ordered a medium pepperoni pie, a 2 liter bottle of Coke, and some Cinnasticks, and was told that the order would take one to two hours to get to the hotel. I was told that I would receive a call when the delivery driver arrived so that I could meet him in the tower's lobby. When I was in the BYOC room earlier that night, I had taken off my name tag since it was getting in the way, so I took it off, but had forgotten to take it with me when I left. So I figured that retrieving my tag would be a good way to kill off some of the one to two hours I would have to wait for the pizza. I was able to get into the BYOC room by showing my Registration Sheet to the front desk there, and agreeing to have someone escort me to CES. I found my name tag under my table hanging on some wires, and when I showed the badge to my escort, he was satisfied and left towards the BYOC's front desk.

> I went back to the tower's lobby to wait for the pizza delivery person to show up, and around 11:30PM, he called me on my cell phone and said he was pulling up to the hotel. When I along with several other QuakeCon attendees met him outside, he was carrying two large cube-shaped bags. We all went back inside, figured out who ordered what, then paid for our meals. I tipped the delivery person two dollars, then took my order back up to my hotel room, but soon after I got there, I realized that I got the wrong side order and not the Cinnasticks I wanted, but it was too late to change things now. I ended the night by having two slices of pepperoni with some Coke, brushing and flossing my teeth, and then going to bed at midnight.
