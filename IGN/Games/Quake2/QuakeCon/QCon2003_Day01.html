QuakeCon 2003 - Day One

> On the 14th of August 2003 (a Thursday), I arrived at Dallas Fort Worth at around 4:00PM Dallas time. I took a ShuttleBus to the Adam's Mark Hotel, where the QuakeCon was being held. I carried all three of my bags (my backpack, laptop bag, and luggage bag) to the second floor, where the BYOC (bring your own own computer) was taking place. While I was signing up, the young lady checking my bags noticed my Pull N Peel Twizzlers, and didn't let me take them into the BYOC room. That seemed odd to me, since food was allowed during the event two years earlier. When I asked someone at the event why that was, he said that the hotel didn't want food in the room, because it could mess up the carpet, while QCon 2001 was held in a room that had a concrete floor.

> The registration folks gave me a sheet of paper they told me not to lose. Not only did it have information I needed to get on the network (IP address settings), I also wouldn't be allowed to take my computer out of the room without it. I left my luggage bag and Twizzlers by the registration tables, took my other two bags into the BYOC room, and settled down at the seventh row from the back of the room, like I did in 2001. The table was apparently reserved for a clan, but since they hadn't shown up yet, the guy next to me suggested that I take the slot for myself, so I did. After putting my bags down on the table, I walked around the room and checked out the different computers people had brought with them, some built from scratch. I recognized some computers from last year, but I did see some new ones as well. I went back to my table, grabbed my digital camera, and took a few pictures of the machines...


> When I returned to my table, I got to work on setting up Glamdring (my IBM ThinkPad), but I couldn't find an Ethernet cable to hook up my machine to the network. So I went up to the big podium at the front of the room, which they called "the Noc", and asked for a cable. They didn't have any left, but they told me to wait 20 minutes for new ones to be crimped. This was only the start of the network problems I'd be having throughout the day. I eventually found an unused Ethernet about 15 feet to the right of me(how the hell did I miss that?), and spent about an hour searching the area around Glamdring for a port to get myself onto the network, but as far as I could see, they were all being used. Then, I realized that the LinkSys Boxes had 16 ports each, and not 8 as I thought, and plugged Glamdring's cable into a nearby port. (On all the boxes I saw, all of the ports on the top row had a plug in them, covering up the bottom row.) Then using my network sheet, I went into my network settings and set them according to the sheet. I had a few false starts, but even after I was sure I set everything correctly, I still couldn't connect to the network. Two of the QuakeCon attendees who knew a lot about networking (or at least more than I did), were kind enough to help me, but even they were stumped. Omitting any further details, I made a few more attempts to get on the network throughout the night, but just didn't have any luck. :( One thing I didn't need the network for was playing Ocarina of Time on UltraHLE, my Nintendo 64 emulator of choice. As I was playing, I did manage to turn a few heads, which felt pretty good. :)

> Sometime that evening, I heard that there were still openings for the Quake3 1v1 tournament, so I rushed to the room where the contest was to be held and signed up for it. Even though I had missed the registration for the tourney when I registered for the QuakeCon a few months earlier, I was still going to compete, and I was just ecstatic. I had to back to my luggage bag to get my keyboard, which was just a regular PS/2, no frills keyboard I got for free as part of my A+ Certification training class nearly a year earlier. I was going to use it for my brand new PS/2 to USB adapter I bought at Radio Shack back in Jersey. But just after 6PM, before I got a chance to use the adapter, someone came up to my table and noticed it plugged into my keyboard, and asked to buy it off me for $20. He said he needed it for one of the contests he was going to compete in. After thinking about it for a while, I reluctantly sold it to him for $19, but wasn't too bummed, since I originally got it for about $14. I eventually checked into the hotel, and was given the keycard for Room 604. I then took my luggage bag and Twizzlers off the hands of the registration people, and brought them up to my room.

> Just before 9PM that day, I began to wonder why my name wasn't being called to announce my turn at the tournament. So I went back to the Quake3 tourney room to find out what the hell was going on. To my horror, my turn to play occurred half an hour earlier, and there was no way I could be rescheduled. It turns out that I was told what time I was going to play when I signed up, and they were not going to announce it in the BYOC room as I'd thought. As you can imagine, I was extremely upset about missing out on the tournament, so I marched back to my table and decided to play on a little Quake over the network, so I could get my mind off of my disappointment. I soon remembered that I couldn't get on the network, so I then decided to play some more Zelda. Everything else is going wrong, but at least I have Zelda to show off, right? Wrong. I clicked on my Desktop shortcut to UltraHLE and noticed the path was missing. So I decided to go into the emulator's directory and open it directly, but I soon discovered my ENTIRE Emulators folder tree was GONE. Then I realized that I'd forgotten to switch to read-only access to my C drive, and some fucking prick must have taken advantage and deleted my directory. For me, that was the last straw and I just lost it. So I called my mother in Aventura and begged her to pick me up the following day and get me the fuck out of there, and if there was no way to take me back to New Jersey, I told her to find a way. But she said my return trip was already booked for Sunday, and then she mentioned something about some big blackout in Jersey or something. She basically said I was stuck there, and I'd have to make the best of things.
