QuakeCon 2003 - Day Three

> Just before 2AM, I was up front at the noc, watching Stepmania up close. All of a sudden, the guys on the noc announced that they were giving away free T-shirts, and then all hell broke loose. A crowd of about a hundred people rushed to the noc at once, like water from a bursting dam. Nash, who was on the noc, tossed the shirts one by one into the crowd, and they grabbed at every shirt. Some staggered and fell as they made some of the bolder grabs, and some of the shorter people stood on top of chairs to get some extra height. I was still up front, trapped in by the crowd, and I was unable to get back to Glamdring until they dispersed. Sometime later, I went back to my room, brushed my teeth, recharged my cell phone, and went to bed just after 4:30AM.

> I woke up at around 10:30AM, took a shower, changed into my Bart Simpson playing Space Invaders T-shirt, and called my mother in Aventura. I told her about my trip so far, and she reminded me to find out when the hotel's check out time was, and to call the ShuttleBus company to take me to the airport the next day. I went down to the lobby to ask if there was a microwave I could use to heat up my now cold pizza, but apparently there wasn't one. I went to the only working vending machine, which was on the fourth floor, but it was out of the sodas that I like. Luckily, some people were selling soda and snacks on the second floor, so I picked up a cold can of Coke for a dollar. I then went to my room, had two slices of cold pizza, then went back to the BYOC room.

> I arrived back at Glamdring just before 1PM, just on time for the second round of the Generations tournament. I played against a player called Dink, and basically got my ass handed to me. I lost 27 frags to -1, but I had a good time nevertheless. My defeat revealed to me how much I really suck at Quake, and that I need to do some substantial practicing if I ever want to become serious tournament material. So I'll try to fit Quake practice into my busy schedule, along with studying for my yet to be scheduled A+ Certification exam, my job at Lady Foot Locker, and tending Creamy's Wonderland.

> The rest of the day was rather uneventful, but entertaining nevertheless. I played a bit more Q3A, and recorded a few of my games, but unfortunately I somehow lost the recordings. I also browsed the network, downloading some songs, and purging one machine of its 30+ viruses with my copy of Norton AntiVirus. After the system was clean, I left a text file on the once-infected computer explaining what I'd done. I even preserved the QuakeCon section of Glamdring's virus log for your viewing pleasure.
