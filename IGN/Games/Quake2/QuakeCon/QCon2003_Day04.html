QuakeCon 2003 - Day Four

> Around 1AM, an announcer on the noc said that we only had 12 hours left before the network was dismantled, and made a joke about this being our last chance to download porn. Somehow, this led to a contest to see who could have the most porn on their system by 2AM. One person had a whopping 197GB of porn on his machine! Soon after the contest started, it was abruptly canceled. I personally think the whole thing was a joke from the start. A few hours later, a video clip was being played on the big screen to the right of the noc. It was a clip of the original 8-bit Super Mario Brothers game, and the player was doing all sorts of crazy things that normally can't be done in the game. In the clip, Mario could swim through spinning rods of fire unharmed, send Koopa Troopas into the ground, and apparently climb on the sides of walls to save himself from falling into pits.

> I think it was around 4 or 5AM when the QuakeCon people treated us to a round of funny movie clips. I had copies of the shown movies already, but this didn't diminish the fun I had watching them. The clips they showed that I didn't have included a cat hanging onto a blade of a spinning ceiling fan, one that showed a group of German factory workers, and a clip of an insanely good Japanese Tetris player. After a few clips, the attendees were allowed to upload their own movies. I decided to upload a few good movies in my collection, and two of them were played; Black White Supremacist, and The Parlor, both of which the crowd enjoyed. However not all the files that were uploaded were welcome. Evidently, some fucking asshole found it necessary to upload 3 viruses onto the server. The guy running the server, communicating to the crowd through WordPad, let us know that he didn't approve of this malicious act. After the movies were finished, I went back to Glamdring and downloaded some of the movies I liked, but didn't have yet.

> After taking a short break to shower, brush teeth, and get about an hour of sleep, I left my room for the final time and checked out of the hotel just before noon. My ShuttleBus was to arrive at 1:30PM, so I figured I'd go back to Glamdring and do some last minute downloading. Since I couldn't get any free T-shirts during my trip, I decided to buy two shirts they were selling in the BYOC room for $20 for two shirts. When I got to my computer, it was out of power, because the power strip it was plugged into was gone, and the machine had run out of power by the time I got to it. So I plugged Glamdring into another strip, hoping to charge the battery enough so I could use the machine on the plane. Just then, I heard an announcement over the loudspeaker that more volunteers were needed to dismantle the network. Since I hadn't yet volunteered for the event, I went up to the noc and offered my assistance. In a little over an hour, I collected a bunch of Ethernet cables, some LinkSys boxes, and 192 bottles of Bawls. Just before 1:30PM, I packed up Glamdring and all my other belongings, when I noticed that someone AGAIN plugged out my machine. I figured it wasn't going to be that much of a big deal, since I could probably plug in at the airport anyway, and have time to charge the battery before the flight arrived.

> A little after 1:30PM, I hopped aboard the ShuttleBus, and was on my to Dallas Fort Worth. I wasn't the only QuakeCon attendee on the bus, though. The creators of PunkBuster were also on their was to the airport, and I talked to them for a while about their program. When I arrived at the airport, I soon discovered, to my horror, that I forgot the yellow envelope containing my e-ticket home to Jersey, and my passport. Mercifully, I was able to get a new e-ticket for free by running my ATM card through a certain machine. Just before I was to go through the metal detector, the security guy noticed my Swiss Army Knife on my keychain, and said I'd have to check it. My problem was that my luggage bag was already on its way to the plane, so my only hope of keeping my knife was to give it to the guy who checks the tickets, and hope he can locate my bag before it got to the plane. He apparently failed, because I never saw my knife again. They wouldn't let my take my knife on the plane, but for some reason, I don't feel any safer whenever I go to the airport. Wonder why. Anyways, I found an outlet to charge Glamdring's battery while I played some FreeCell, and I got it up to 94% before I had to pack up and board my flight home.
