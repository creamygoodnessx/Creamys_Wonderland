QuakeCon 2001 - Day Two

> We woke up the next morning around 9 or 9:30. I visited my parents room (204) to see how they were doing. Before heading back to the QCon, Goatman and I enjoyed a quick breakfast at Comfort, then went shopping in town. I got myself a couple of two liters of Pepsi and some candy, and when we arrived at the QCon, we parted ways. I managed to get a few downloads started when the people at the podium requested volunteers for something. So I figured, 'what the hell, if these guys are offering us this free event, why not give back something in return.' I went up to the podium to see what they needed, and they asked me to watch a door, to make sure that no unauthorized people went through, until a cop showed up. While I was standing there, my parents arrived and offered to take me to a rodeo later that night. I accepted their offer, even though rodeos aren't my thing. When I was finally relieved from the guard position, Nash from the QCon staff upgraded my name tag from 'General' to 'Staff'. Later on in the day, I helped to check configs at the tournament. Right after I finished, I met up with Hunter and spoke with him for a couple of minutes before heading back to Glamdring to try out the Generations mod. Later that night, I went to my first rodeo, and while I felt like I was in some kind of alien world and even uncomfortable at times, I never regretted going. Conveniently, the rodeo was located next door to the QCon. When I returned to my laptop, I tried to get onto a particular Generations server, but Glamdring spent over an hour downloading maps, so I didn't get to play too much of it. Of course, I managed to sneak in some Quake2, as I had noticed the two people on my right were playing it. It was a lot of fun, as my two opponents were only slightly stronger than me. Like the previous night, Goatman and I returned to the Comfort Inn late.
