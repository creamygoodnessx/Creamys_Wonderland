* No frills version of Config File Setup *

    DEADLINE: 10/20/2K - Probably the most challenging task of Tier 4, and definitely the most important, I plan to build a draft version of the CFS movie in Animation: Master. A few shots will be left out in order to help me to push this draft movie out the door ASAP. Differences between the draft version of CFS and its future completed version are as follows. There will be no sound at all, and the draft will lack the character lip movement the final version will have. There will be no civilian extras in the draft, and the scenes will be built with only the parts of the background that interact with Brandy and Jatoten, such as the benches in the shuttlecar. With the exception of the elevator and its doors, textures will almost be completely left out of the draft. The soldiers in the cut scene will be temporarily played by some of the models on the AM2000 CD.

> As time and the tiers go on, I will gradually add in the different elements of the Config File Setup movie, starting with the voices in the next tier, lip sync in the sixth, as well as a few others. Each scene will be its own AVI file. Even with two extra days per task in this tier, it wouldn't surprise me if this task causes me to fall behind again.

> After pondering this issue for the past couple days, I've finally decided what the two criteria will be for any version of BJQ2T to be considered full (v1.x). First, the episode must include all of the crucial parts of each scene, and be able to tell the entire story accurately in it's present from (not including sound). The only parts of any scene that may be omitted are the ones which exist purely as a supplement for the scene's important parts. For example, Scene 4 of Config File Setup is a four-parter, and is about a keyboarder who gets gang raped by more experienced players. The first part alone is enough to get the point across that keyboarders are usually the prime targets during a deathmatch, while the other three parts are there to emphasize this point, and to add further excitement to the episode. Therefore, only the first part is really needed in order to make CFS an official full episode. The second criteria for a full episode is that all of the crucial voice tracks are included.
