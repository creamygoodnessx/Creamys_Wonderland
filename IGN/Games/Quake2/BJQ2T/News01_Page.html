It took the use of Animation: Master, Paint Shop Pro, Adobe After Effects, and Adobe Premiere to pull this off. Instead of trying to complete the entire no-frills CFS in a single tier like I've been trying to do for the past two tiers, I'll try to focus on one scene of the episode per tier. This may be a wise decision since Scene 6 of CFS is a seven-parter, and the final scene of the episode involves something I've never experimented with before. As for the other tasks in Tier 6, I plan to post a few concept sketches of the first extra character, and do some of the voice overs for the first three scenes.

10/21/2K - The first part of Scene 4 of Config File Setup is now available in its first draft form.

7/1/2K - It now looks like I'm a little behind on this project. Sometimes, it's amazing how fast a commanding lead over your schedule can dissolve before your eyes.  I spent the past few days busting my ass on a project for school, but now that the semester is over, I can finally move on to what's really important.

6/4/2K - A few days ago, I began work on a new project. In an effort to eventually work up the will to begin future projects on my Into the Fray Universe, I've decided to start an Internet series of a different kind. It will be a strategic and tactical guide to Quake2, aptly named Brandy and Jatoten's Quake2 Tactics. The show will consist of several Quicktime movies, and will cover everything from rocket jumping to setting up config files. This new project should be easier that ITF:HON for two reasons. First of all, I'll require far less help than I did for ITF:HON. I will need someone to play the voice of Brandy Hothiegh, but other than that and some occasional advice on a few things, I'll going to be just fine. Second, I'm giving myself ample time to finish the project, a month for each tier to be exact. Even with school and the possibility of getting a new job, I should be able to finish the tiers on time.

> The BJQ2T project will consist of five one month-long tiers, each consisting of a certain number of tasks. Much like the ITF:HON project, the tasks in each tier will remain in place until completed or until the tier is finished. If time on any given tier expires, its tasks will carry over to the next tier, and must be completed in addition to the tasks that tier. If a tier is completed significantly early, I'll give myself a day or two off from the project, then begin the next tier with its original deadline still in place. In other words, I'll have even more time to work on each task of that tier, and will be way ahead of schedule. If I complete all five tiers, I will take start to take it easy on BJQ2T, and work on it without any further official scheduling.
