Quake 2: Old News
(12/20/2K to 7/3/2001)

10/2/2002 (Follycount) - I have decided to abort the BJQ2T project, and focus on developing the Follycount series instead. However, BJQ2T was not a total waste, as there is a lot from this project I can salvage for use Follycount. I've re-aranged this page so that it focuses on the Config File Setup episode of BJQ2T, rather than remaining open for any future development. I don't know how this page is going to turn out in the future, but it's a safe bet that this will not be its final overhaul.

> It may be hard to believe, but it's been exactly one year since I paid any attention to this section of my site. During this time, I've been focusing on the development of Evil Blood, as well as taking care of my academic needs at Bergen. My primary problem with developing Follycount is that I've never been satisfied with its characters. It's not easy for me to achieve a cast with a balance of personalities of people I know in real life, and stereotypical characters on the Internet, not to mention deciding what to do with what's left of Brandy and Jatoten. I already have a few story ideas in mind, but without a cast of characters, there's just not much I can do with them. That's why I've decided to officially suspend the Follycount project, at least until I can come up with some decent characters. I've also taken down the characters page, and it will remain unavailable until the cast is fully re-assembled. When I eventually do get Follycount up and running again, I'll make damn sure it was well worth the wait. Trust me.

10/2/2001 (Follycount) - There have been some critical changes to the Follycount series. Rather than have the series revolve around a single virtual chat room, I've decided to follow a theme that resembles my old BJQ2T project. Now, Follycount will be just one of six Quake clans the story of Follycount covers, and some of the characters Clan Follycount have been relocated to these other clans. I'm also bringing back a few of my old characters from the old BJQ2T. In addition, I've made a few changes to the descriptions of some of the characters, and eliminated a few characters as well. As for the minor changes, I've fixed a few wayward links in the BJQ2T section.

7/3/2001 (Follycount) - I'm almost ready to begin actively working on Follycount as an Internet series, but first, there is one thing I have to take care of before that happens. The first Follycount movie I plan on making will be one of my trailers, although I haven't yet decided on exactly which one I'll be working on. Whichever one it ends up being, I'll have to come up with a detailed script for it, much like the one for Config File Setup. Once the script is done, Tier 1 of Follycount will commence, officially getting this damn thing off the ground.

6/4/2K (Follycount) - A few days ago, I began work on a new project. In an effort to eventually work up the will to begin future projects on my Into the Fray Universe, I've decided to start an Internet series of a different kind. It will be a strategic and tactical guide to Quake2, aptly named Brandy and Jatoten's Quake2 Tactics. The show will consist of several Quicktime movies, and will cover everything from rocket jumping to setting up config files. This new project should be easier that ITF:HON for two reasons. First of all, I'll require far less help than I did for ITF:HON. I will need someone to play the voice of Brandy Hothiegh, but other than that and some occasional advice on a few things, I'll going to be just fine. Second, I'm giving myself ample time to finish the project, a month for each tier to be exact. Even with school and the possibility of getting a new job, I should be able to finish the tiers on time.

> The BJQ2T project will consist of five one month-long tiers, each consisting of a certain number of tasks. Much like the ITF:HON project, the tasks in each tier will remain in place until completed or until the tier is finished. If time on any given tier expires, its tasks will carry over to the next tier, and must be completed in addition to the tasks that tier. If a tier is completed significantly early, I'll give myself a day or two off from the project, then begin the next tier with its original deadline still in place. In other words, I'll have even more time to work on each task of that tier, and will be way ahead of schedule. If I complete all five tiers, I will take start to take it easy on BJQ2T, and work on it without any further official scheduling.
