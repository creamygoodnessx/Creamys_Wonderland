Quake 2 PPMs

> I became interested in the art of modeling Quake 2 plugin player models (PPMs) when I first visited the Q2PMP in the middle of 1998. From that moment, I became a PPM junkie, collecting good, high quality models wherever I could find them. I always wanted to get into PPM modeling myself, and had a few ideas from a few of my interests in other genres, but lacked the tools and know-how to do so. It was the middle of the July of 1999 when I got my first real break. After almost a year of searching for the right tutorials and software, I was finally ready to start my first model, Chuckie Finster.

3/20/2003 - From this point forward, I will post all Quake PPM works in progress on this page, so that you can see the progress I'm making with each one. There are two models for Quake2 I'm currently working on. Creamy Goodness, formerly known as Jatoten Rascot Battle Mage, is a model I completed quite a while back, but only now am I finding the time to put it into a game environment. If the updates on the model's page look familiar, that's because CG was part of two projects I was doing a while back. After I finish CG as a Quake model, I probably won't do any more major work on it.

> Over a year and a half ago, I did a poll on the front page of Creamy's Wonderland, asking what cartoon character you would like to see as a Quake2 or Quake3 model. The winner of that poll was Dib, and I'm sure that most people who visit this site regularly (all 12 of you) were starting to doubt if I'd ever get around to getting Dib into the arenas. Well, as it turns out, I finally had the chance to do just that, and you can now see the model as it gets built step by step.
