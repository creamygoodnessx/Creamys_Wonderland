Quake 2: Old News
(2/11/2K to 8/1/2K)

8/1/2K (Follycount) - Well, well, well. Another month, another tier. Compared to the last two tiers, Tier 3 shouldn't be too much of a problem. Config File Setup will be the first movie of the project, and my biggest obstacle now is finding someone to play the voice of Brandy.

7/27/2K (Follycount) - It's near the end of Tier 2, and I've decided to make a last minute change to my schedule. Tier 3 will revolve around the development of first version of my first movie in the tactical guide. It will focus on config files, what they are, and the method I use to write them. Because of the trouble I've been having on the Jatoten Rascot Battle Mage VWEP, and because of the new direction I'm taking the project, I will suspend the development of the VWEP.

7/1/2K (Follycount) - It now looks like I'm a little behind on this project. Sometimes, it's amazing how fast a commanding lead over your schedule can dissolve before your eyes. I spent the past few days busting my ass on a project for school, but now that the semester is over, I can finally move on to what's really important.

6/4/2K (Follycount) - A few days ago, I began work on a new project. In an effort to eventually work up the will to begin future projects on my Into the Fray universe, I've decided to start an Internet series of a different kind. It will be a strategic and tactical guide to Quake2, aptly named Brandy and Jatoten's Quake2 Tactics. The show will consist of several Quicktime movies, and will cover everything from rocket jumping to setting up config files. This new project should be easier that ITF:HON for two reasons. First of all, I'll require far less help than I did for ITF:HON. I will need someone to play the voice of Brandy Hothiegh, but other than that and some occasional advice on a few things, I'll going to be just fine. Second, I'm giving myself ample time to finish the project, a month for each tier to be exact. Even with school and the possibility of getting a new job, I should be able to finish the tiers on time.

> The BJQ2T project will consist of five one month-long tiers, each consisting of a certain number of tasks. Much like the ITF:HON project, the tasks in each tier will remain in place until completed or until the tier is finished. If time on any given tier expires, its tasks will carry over to the next tier, and must be completed in addition to the tasks that tier. If a tier is completed significantly early, I'll give myself a day or two off from the project, then begin the next tier with its original deadline still in place. In other words, I'll have even more time to work on each task of that tier, and will be way ahead of schedule. If I complete all five tiers, I will take start to take it easy on BJQ2T, and work on it without any further official scheduling.

4/26/2K (PPMs) - Finally, 73 days since I started work on my Tony Toponi Quake2 PPM, he's finished. Not only is his VWEP completed, but his skin is also darker in order to show his eyes, which before where blended into Tony's washed-out skin in OpenGL mode.

4/17/2K (PPMs) - After 18 days of busting my ass on a fully animated hyperblaster weapon model, I'm proud to present Version 0.3 of the Tony Toponi PPM. This version also includes animations for Tony's rocket launcher, but only on the attack animations.

3/30/2K (PPMs) - Its been less than 24 hours since I last updated IWOW, and already the Tony model is upgraded to Beta Version 0.2. 3/29/2K (PPMs) - I sent in the latest version of my Tony model on the 21st of this month. As it turns out, the deadline of the contest was the 20th of March, not February. Anyways, my entry is in, and the first beta version of the Tony Toponi Quake2 PPM is now available on this site. His VWEP is only partially completed, consisting of only 8 out of 12 weapon models. Sometime in the future, I plan to model the 4 remaining weapons, and animate some of them as well. Once this is done, I'll upgrade Tony to Version 1.1, which will be the post-beta version of the model.
