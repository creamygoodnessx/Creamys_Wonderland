Binky

 - CHARACTER STATS -
Real Name - ?
Race - Gwahrai
Residence - The Gwahrai Forest
Demeanor - Reckless, hyperactive, crafty, but immature
Age - 14 (approx. 9 in human years)
Height/Weight - 2' 2"? / 12lbs?
Languages - Gwahrai (fluent), primary elven language (understood but not spoken)

> Binky is a preteen gwahrai who lives in a treetop village in the middle of a forest close to Nnet Olotor's equator. She is a high-spirited bundle of energy who does not seem to have an "off switch". She is exceptionally curious, even by gwahrai standards, and has a natural flair for adventure. This curiosity often gets her into trouble, because she often sneaks away from the safety of the village to explore the wilderness alone, sometimes getting herself into life-threatening situations. In such cases, her adopted brother Oinkillune has to find her, rescue her if necessary, and then escort her home.

> But sneaking out of town isn't the only kind of trouble Binky gets herself into. She is extremely reckless and does not understand the difference between acts of courage and foolish risks. She regularly tries to 'prove' her supposed bravery by performing dangerous stunts. Sometimes, Binky and her friends like to anger dangerous wild animals, and provoke the animals into attacking them. (This could be a gwahrai equivalent of 'cow-tipping'.) There may be instances where Binky's stunts went horribly wrong, and would have resulted in her death if Oinkillune hadn't jumped in and saved her. But no matter how many times Binky barely escapes death from her foolish stunts, she doesn't appear to learn from her ways, and keeps getting into trouble over and over again.

> After Binky and Oinkillune leave the forest for the first time in their lives, Binky soon discovers herself feeling lethargic, and not nearly as energetic as she normally is. She, Oinkillune, and Jonny-V eventually discover that the food outside the Gwahrai Forest is not ideal for gwahrai consumption, and does not contain the nutrients to keep Binky in perfect health. Because of this, the party has to return to the forest periodically to get a fresh supply of Gwahrai leaves and berries to fully maintain Binky's energy. They may find an acceptable substitute for this food or find a way to grow the forest's food outside the forest, but I'll tackle that issue as I further develop the story.

> Binky, like most of Evil Blood's characters, goes through a number of personality changes over the course of the story. She begins thinking she can control any situation with ease, and feeling like she's invincible. However, I will probably have her abruptly realise that there are obstacles in the world too great for her to handle, and that the good guys don't always win. I may do this by putting her up against a far superior enemy, who thrashes Binky and keeps knocking her down every time she engages him. This could be a major wake up call for Binky that causes her to handle situations more carefully. There will of course be other events that help to further reshape Binky, although I don't what those situations are just yet. I will most likely put her in a certain situation late in the story that tests her new personality, kind of like a final exam if you will. In fact, I can do something that demonstrates how much each of the characters have evolved, be it in the same episode or in one for each character.
