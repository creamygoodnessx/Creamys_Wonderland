Oinkillune
(2/22/2002 to 8/30/2002)

 - CHARACTER STATS -
Real Name - Oinkillune
Race - Elven
Residence - ?
Demeanor - Courageous, Selfless, Heroic
Age - 27 (approx. 14 in human years)
Height/Weight - 5' 1" / 105lbs
Alignment - Lawful Good

> Oinkillune is a young teenage elf who was adopted by a gwahrai couple from his infancy, and lives with them in their home in a village in the woods. He is someone with a high degree of ethical standards, and puts the needs of others ahead of his own, often going out of his way to help total strangers in need without expecting anything in return. Despite being drastically physically different from his gwahrai peers, he has no trouble fitting into their society, and has become an excellent tree climber despite not having a tail or hands for feet. He values the well being of his family and community over all things, even though he has yet to fully appreciate what they mean to him. Oinkillune has a somewhat feminine personality, and because of his high voice, slender frame, and sometimes girly mannerisms, he's often mistaken by strangers for a girl. He has a gentleman-like and magnetic personality, tends to get along with everyone he meets, and never looks down upon people.

> Oinkillune considers his biggest challenge to be looking after his 13 year old (approximately nine in human years) sister Binky. Binky is exceptionally curious, even by gwahrai standards, and does not know the difference between an act of bravery and a foolish risk. She is extremely reckless and must be watched at all times in order to be kept out of harm's way. Binky's reckless actions have included things like challenging predatory beasts to one-on-one fights, and attempting dangerous stunts, but her brother has always been fortunate enough to consistently save Binky from her own suicidal nature.

> Until recently, Oinkillune was a staunch pacifist and shunned violence, and never had to resort to it. In the village where he lived, pacifism is not an unwise lifestyle, since a great majority of the community's population have good ethics and respond well to reason. He had always felt uneasy carrying around his gwahrai thornstick and sling.Like most of the other residents of the village, he learned to use these two weapons as part of his adopted people's tradition, but never took armed combat seriously and doubted if he could bring himself to use them for lethal force if he needed to. However, Oinkillune suffered a traumatic event three years ago that would forever change his pacifist ways. While traveling one day with Binky, the two were confronted by a small group of human thugs who demanded their money. Having never been in a violent confrontation, Oinkillune basically told the thugs to behave themselves and go home, as if they were some of the small children who made trouble back in the village. (He made this demand in Elven, since he assumed the thugs could not speak his native Gwahrai, and since he didn't speak any English.) The thugs attacked Oinkillune and nearly killed him, but Binky managed to throw down a smokestick she had been carrying, which allowed her and her brother to escape. (Where Binky got the smokestick and how she learned to use it is another issue altogether, and is something which will be explained on her character page.) (I may or may not eventually change the traumatic scenario to some other type of violent encounter, but the impact is has on Oinkillune will be the same.)

> Before the incident, Oinkillune had regularly heard stories about violent criminals, but had always refused to believe that anyone could actually be capable of that kind of violence, and had always adhered to his belief that pacifism was the best solution to all confrontations. For the first time in his life, he began to understand and accept how dangerous people can be, and that sometimes it's necessary to fight back, but he still had yet to fathom the true extremes of evil a person can posses, such as the evil within Zod and Emperor Stevens. He used the incident to warn the people in his community to not make the same mistake he made in denying the existence of evil, and reminded them that self defense is a life saving skill that should never be taken lightly. In an effort to make up for neglecting his self defense skills, Oinkillune began taking his fighting skills a lot more seriously with not only the gwahrai weapons he grew up with, but with a few elven weapons that gwahrai commonly use as well, and even went as far as to seek the aid of non-gwahrai warriors for practice. (I'm not sure if he takes this training a bit further by joining one of Nnet Olotor's armies as well, but I'll wait for other elements of the story to develop further before making that decision.)

> In the three years following the attack that nearly killed him, Oinkillune developed a strong hatred towards bullies and people who use their power (political power, size, etc.) to forcefully bend others to their will. He never backs down to a bully, nor does he allow anyone to talk down to him, but his hatred often clouds his judgment so that he forgets when he's overmatched. Other than this desire to preserve his pride, he does not suffer any other lapses in his judgement when it comes to violent confrontations, and respects his physical limitations. Even though his contempt for violence has not wavered, he won't hesitate to use it if need be. However, Oinkillune still doubts that he could ever use lethal force if he had to, and never carries his thornstick with the thorn attached.

> Like most Nnet Olotorians, Oinkillune keeps track of the war between the Stevens Empire and Nnet Olotor, but he never bothered to learn too much about Earthen culture and traditions. From the time he first heard about the war, he had assumed that the evil ways of the Stevens Empire was the norm for the entire human race, and this prejudice was reinforced by the attack on him and Binky by the human thugs. As a result, Oinkillune has a great fear and distrust towards the human race, not just the Stevens Empire, and only the few good humans he has met on occasion prevents him from thinking that the entire race is inherently and totally evil. During his travels, Oinkillune avoids human cities whenever he can, since he assumes that such places are hotbeds of violent crime. Since his contact with humans is very limited, he has never bothered to learn their languages. Still, when Oinkillune deals with unfamiliar but seemingly friendly Elven speaking humans, he tries to put his prejudices aside and let his true, curtious nature shine through.

> During the past few weeks, Oinkillune started having strange nightmares, in which the evil deity Zod pleaded with him to 'free' him, and promising the young elf great power if he did. The nightmares were getting progressively worse, and Oinkillune thought he might be going insane, so he told his family about the dreams. They insisted that he visit the Orb of Knowledge in order to get some answers.
