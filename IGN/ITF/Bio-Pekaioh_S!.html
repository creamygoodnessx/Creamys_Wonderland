Pekaioh
(Spoiler)

> During his adventures with Jonny-V's party, Pekaioh notices that his companions are extremely powerful fighters. Travelling with a creation of Zod makes him feel very uncomfortable, and he starts to become jealous of Oinkillune when he sees that his fighting prowess is greater than his own. No matter how much Pekaioh trains or what new techniques he learns, he always seems to be one step behind Oinkillune, and as the series continues, his jealousy towards Oinkillune grows. After Oinkillune defeats a seemingly invincible enemy, Pekaioh sees that the Zodian angel's fighting potential far exceeds anything that he can hope to accomplish, and this turns out to be a major turning point for the reincarnated hero. He secretly and desparately seeks out ways to surpass Oinkillune, and is willing to do so at any cost. Pekiaoh eventually sells his soul to Zod, the very being his predecessor fought so hard to destroy, and turns his back on everyone that cared about him, and everyone who looked up to him. His burning jealously and anger towards Oinkillune, combined with Zod's corrosive influence, robs him of whatever conscience he has left. However, Pekiaoh's trecherous act grants him more power than he could have ever wanted, and allows him to finally surpass Oinkillune, although Oinkillune or Jonny-V will most likely kill him by the end of the series. I may have Pekaioh become one of Zod's most prized champions, and a top ranking officer in his army. Because Pekiaoh violated every rule in his magic order by joining Zod, he was most likely stripped of his inborn magic abilities by his creator, but then granted a substantial amount of Zodian magic energy by his new master. Although Pekaioh became completely evil, his work ethic remained solid, and through his hard work and patience, it didn't take him long to master a few Zodian spells.

The Original Intended Apprentice

> Zod may have sought after Pekaioh after learning about his jealousy towards Oinkillune, and immediately made plans to include him in a special place within his ranks. But Pekaioh may not have been Zod's first choice to take this special place. Zod may have had his eye on Jonny-V since she first became a mage. He figured that Jonny-V would be perfect for his army, because of her fighting potential and her apparent willingness to single handedly bring down her father's empire at all costs. However, Jonny-V refused to serve Zod, since it would mean giving up the freedom she risked her life to gain from her father, and she felt that doing so was too high a price to pay for some extra power. She also may have felt that she could only satisfy her thirst for vengeance by defeating the Stevens Empire on her own, and receiving any outside help would cheapen the experience. This forced Zod to find another lackey, but he wouldn't be able to find one until years later when he discovered Pekaioh.
