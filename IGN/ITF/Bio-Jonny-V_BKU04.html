> Genevieve Alexandria Stevens is the daughter of Earthen conqueror Emperor Stevens. She once had the potential of being a nice and sociable person, but an unkind childhood changed all that. Genevieve grew up in the Stevens Empire, a society dominated by idealism, in which its members are judged primarily by their social class, gender, and physical appearance. When Genevieve reached her adolecence, she did not fit the empire's ideals as a good looking young lady, and was given a very hard time for it from most of the people she knew, including her father. The empire is also heavily male-oriented, in which men get to make all the important decisions and hold positions of power, while women are meant to be seen and not heard, and are given far fewer rights than male counterparts. Genevieve refused to accept herself an an inferior, and always questioned the empire's gender biased views. She repeatedly did things that were only acceptable for men to do, such as carrying a weapon, and wearing certain attire, which caused further resentment of her. Because the Stevens Empire looked down on the practice of magic as witchcraft, Genevieve's open fascination with magic only made her even more of an outcast. Noone in Genevieve's childhood showed any compassion towards her, nor did they ever take anything she said or did seriously. Whenever she tried to speak her mind in a serious manner, it was always passed off as a joke. Genevieve spent her early years toeing the line for her father and peers, and did everything she could to please them, but all she ever got in return was disdain and ungratefulness. Whenever Genevieve tried to resolve a personal dispute with her father in a diplomatic and peaceful way, her efforts would always be thrown back in her face with hostility, and her father would remind her of how worthless she was. Every time such an incident occurred, it would stick into her memory for life, and permanently erode her self esteem.

> Genevieve's only salvation came in the form of an elven slave who, like Genevieve, was an outcast to society, simply because of his race and social status. This slave idea is still reletively new, so I haven't yet come up with a name for him yet. Because the two were outcasts, it wasn't difficult for them to form a strong emotional bond. When Genevieve was twelve, she had once stood up for the slave, and protected him from a beating from his cruel owners, so in return, the slave secretly spent his free time teaching her how to speak, read, and write his language as a sign of gratitude. Although the slave could speak English, he had a strong accent from his native language, which was Nnet Olotor's most common elven language. As a result, the slave kept mispronouncing Genevieve's name as "Johnny V". Genevieve took advantage of her new language abilities by reading elven literature and history. She also looked up everything she could about Nnet Olotor's various magic orders, and what she could do to someday become a mage herself. However, the Stevens Empire forbids the use of magic by law, and the penalty for violating this law is death. So, at the age of 16, Genevieve finally defected from the empire, to persue her lifelong dream of becoming a mage. I'm not yet sure what happened to the slave. It's possible that Emporer Stevens brutally murdered him, because he discovered the truth and felt that the slave was corrupting his daughter, and that that was the final straw that pushed Genevieve to leave home. Or perhaps Genevieve saw a once in a lifetime opportunity to help the slave to escape the empire, and decided it was a good idea for her to leave as well. This part of the story is still very much up in the air.
