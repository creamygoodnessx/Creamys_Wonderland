Evil Blood: The Zodian Threat
(Spoiler)

> Zod never completely ran himself out of magic energy, and therefore, never really died. Instead, he was forced into a mortal shell which bore the likeness of whatever his worshippers believed him to look like. When he cast the spell that created his angels, he saved the last bit of magic energy to keep him alive when he retreated into his physical body. Since Zod is one of Nnet Olotor's evil deities, he gains magic energy whenever his worshippers pay homage to him by committing acts of evil, which usually take the form of the sacrifice of a person. Normally, Zod can only gain magic energy when his followers commit such acts. However, when he was trapped in his physical body, he could gain magic energy from any violent death on Nnet Olotor, regardless of who was responsible for any such death. Despite this tremendous advantage to his interests, Zod only used took a bodily form as a last resort for three important reasons. First, while in his physical body, Zod would no longer be able to keep a constant eye on his worshippers (an ability that many other deities have), thus limiting his communicative abilities to person to person contact. Second, his magic order would be significantly weaker, dramatically decreasing the effectiveness of the spells his worshippers cast. Also, because Zod's magic energy was now eminating from a single point (Zod's body), rather than uniformly from all over the planet, his magic order got weaker as it got further away from his fortress. Third and most importantly, as a mortal, Zod would be subject to every means of death as any mortal creature, and if he was killed while in his physical body, he would be permanently destroyed along with his magic order. In order to return to full strength, Zod would need tens of thousands of violent deaths, and once this is achieved, he would only need the blood of one of his angels to return to his deity form, giving him another chance to wreak havoc on Nnet Olotor. Although violent deaths on Nnet Oloter give Zod strength, they are not necessary for him to ultimately return to his deity form. Zod could opt to abandon his physical body before he reaches full strength, but it would mean having to then get his magic energy back solely by rebuilding his religious community, wasting the violent deaths as part of his plan.

> Besides his lack of magic power, Zod also faced a different problem. During the war against Nnet Olotor's good armies, a great majority of his followers had been killed. Because Zod, like any other deity, relies on the number and loyalty of his worshippers not only to maintain the power of his magic order, but also to stay alive while he's in his deity form, he would need to reestablish his religious community before his return to his deity form. If Zod does not have a considerable base of followers by the time of his return to his deity form, he will gradually lose magic energy after his return, until the amount of his energy once again matches the energy the number and loyalty of his worshippers can provide. I'll have to come up with a way for his followers to start some kind of recruiting campaign to attract new worshippers. I'm not sure how to do this, but the current situation with the Al Quiada may give me a few pointers.

> Because 90 percent of Zod's army had been wiped out, he obviously would have a hard time converting new worshippers, let alone having them carry out the tens of thousands of deaths he needed to return to full strength. However, Zod had anticipated this setback long before his defeat, and since then had come up with a countermeasure. Right after his defeat, he had his followers start a false rumor throughout Nnet Olotor's civilizations that any one of his angels were inherently evil, and could resurrect the evil deity if they chose to do so, and only they knew how to do it. It was Zod's hope that this rumor would trigger a public witch hunting campaign in which enough people would be killed to satisfy Zod's kill quota. The public is not aware of the existence of Zod's physical body, the advantages it gives him, or even the fact that he's still alive. If they were, they would not hunt down Zod's children, but would rather go after Zod himself. So needless to say, the existence of Zod's physical body is a secret that Zod only shares with his most trusted worshippers.

> However, things didn't go exactly according to Zod's plan. Most of the elves were appalled at the idea of killing their own children, even though they 'knew' what the alternative was. Instead, they searched for ways to purge the evil they were led to believe was lurking within their children. However, to Zod's good fortune, one Nnet Olotorian faction was not so compassionate, and felt they needed to do whatever it took to stop Zod from once again defiling their world. So they began to hunt down the children of Zod themselves, managing to slaughter around 300 of their targets, along with the people trying to defend them. The genocidal faction was eventually stopped, and as punishment for their actions, was banished from the mainland.

> Although the deity of knowledge could read the thoughts and secrets of everyone on Nnet Olotor, he could not read those of other deities. This made it possible for Zod to keep his secrets safe from the god of knowledge, including whatever plans he had for his return to power. However, Zod would eventually have to tell at least a few of his followers about his secret if they were to carry out his plans, risking discovery. Long before he ever had to worry about being overthrown, he had his followers build a fortress in an uninhabited and isolated part of the world, and had them install magic inhibitors throughout the building. These inhibitors were designed to block out the magic energy of all deities except Zod, so that only his magic order could exist within the building. Because the god of knowledge's mind reading ability required any recipients to be in contact with his own magic energy, he could not pick up the thoughts of anyone within the boundaries of Zod's fortress, making it the ideal place for Zodians to hold secret meetings. Needless to say, Zod never told anyone about his most classified information until the completion of the fortress. Zod did not worry about the god of knowledge discovering the truth about his plans for his return to the deity form. The false rumors Zod was spreading throughout Nnet Olotor in order to incite its population to hunt down and kill his angels also fooled the deity of knowledge. This is because the deity of knowledge's only information about the mortal world was what he read from people's thoughts, and the only people who knew the truth were confined within the walls of Zod's fortress. Also, any campaign to destroy the Orb of Knowledge would be in vain, because its deity can always create a new one in its place.

5/20/2003 - About a week ago, I came up with a new idea for Evil Blood's plot, an idea that might have been influenced by DragonBall Z. I was thinking about the kinds of spells that might be appropriate for the Zodian magic order. They would have to be spells that only someone who is evil would normally cast, and could be based on death, anguish, and suffering. I thought of a few ideas for spells, most if not all of them already exist in Dungeons and Dragons. One of these ideas included a giant symbol that would immediately kill anyone who looked directly at it. Then, I remembered a particular episode of DBZ in which a super-powerful villain called Majin Buu wiped out most of the Earth's human population with a single attack which he called the Human Extinction Attack. I thought that such a technique would be perfect for Zod's magic order, so I decided to put my own spin on it. Eventually, I came up with a possible spell called the Global Extinction spell, that would kill all members of a chosen species throughout the world.

> I soon realized that I could apply such a spell to Evil Blood's story. After the Stevens Empire gains a significant foothold on Nnet Olotor, some of the world's good armies may feel that all humans are inherently evil, and must be wiped off the face of the planet altogether. Desperate, they may turn to the Global Extinction spell in order to achieve their goal. However, the spell exists only in the Zodian magic order. As you can imagine, the Global Extinction spell is one of Zod's most powerful spells, and demands an unimaginable amount of magic energy to cast, and even 28 years after Zod's defeat, there still may not be enough magic energy in the entire order to cast such a spell. So any party seeking to use this method to purge the humans from Nnet Olotor may try to help Zod's magic order regain its former level of power. This idea is not yet being officially implemented into EB's plot, and I'm not sure if it ever will be, but it just gives me something to think about.
