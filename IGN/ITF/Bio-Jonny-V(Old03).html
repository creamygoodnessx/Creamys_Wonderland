Jonny-V
(10/2/2004 to 2/18/2006)

 - CHARACTER STATS -
Real Name - Genevieve Alexandria Stevens
Race - Human
Residence - None: drifter
Demeanor - Hostile, stubborn, bitter, vengeful, but hard-working and courageous
Age - 20
Height/Weight - 5' 4" / 170lbs
Alignment - Chaotic Evil

> Genevieve Alexandria Stevens is the daughter of Earthen conqueror Emperor Stevens. She once had the potential of being a nice and sociable person, but an unkind childhood changed all that. Genevieve grew up in the Stevens Empire, a society dominated by idealism, in which its members are judged primarily by their social class, gender, and physical appearance. She did not fit the empire's ideals as a good looking young lady, and was given a very hard time for it from most of the people she knew, mainly her father and his elitist peers. (The ideal look for a woman in the Steven's Empire is to have a tall and slender body, and a youthful appearance, but Genevieve was a short and very heavy set girl in her pre-adolescence.)  The empire is also heavily male-oriented, in which men get to make all the important decisions and hold positions of power, while women are meant to be seen and not heard, and are given far fewer rights than male counterparts. Genevieve refused to accept herself as an inferior, and always questioned the empire's gender biased views. She repeatedly did things that were only acceptable for men to do, such as carrying a weapon, and wearing certain male attire, which earned her further resentment. Because the Stevens Empire looked down on the practice of magic as witchcraft, Genevieve's open fascination with magic only made her even more of an outcast. Noone in Genevieve's childhood showed any compassion towards her, nor did they ever take anything she said or did seriously. Whenever she tried to speak her mind in a serious manner, it was always passed off as a joke. Her father didn't even care that she stood in the war room and listened in on his private military meetings with his generals, top military advisors, and his Zodian allies, and he never thought that Genevieve might someday turn on him. Genevieve spent her early years toeing the line for her father and peers, and did everything she could to please them, but all she ever got in return was disdain and ungratefulness. Whenever Genevieve tried to resolve a personal dispute with her father in a diplomatic and peaceful way, her efforts would always be thrown back in her face with hostility, and her father would remind her of how worthless she was. Every time such an incident occurred, it would stick into her memory for life, and permanently erode her ever diminishing good nature.

> Genevieve's only salvation came in the form of an elven slave named Kailaynah who, like Genevieve, was an outcast to society, simply because of his race and social status. Because the two were outcasts, it wasn't difficult for them to form a strong emotional bond. When Genevieve was nine, she stood up for Kailaynah and protected him from a beating from his cruel owners. In return, Kailaynah secretly spent his free time teaching her how to speak, read, and write his language as a sign of gratitude. Although Kailaynah could speak English, he had a strong accent from his native language, which was Nnet Olotor's most common elven language. As a result, he kept mispronouncing Genevieve's name as "Johnny V". Genevieve took advantage of her new language abilities by reading elven literature and history. She also looked up everything she could about Nnet Olotor's various magic orders, and what she could do to someday become a mage herself. However, the Stevens Empire forbids the use of magic by law, and the penalty for violating this law is death. So, at the age of 12, Genevieve finally mustered up the courage to defect from the empire, to pursue her lifelong dream of becoming a mage. I'm not yet sure what happened to Kailaynah. It's possible that Emperor Stevens brutally murdered him, because he discovered the truth and felt that he was corrupting his daughter, and that was the final straw that pushed Genevieve to leave home. Or perhaps Genevieve saw a once in a lifetime opportunity to help him to escape the empire, and decided it was a good idea for her to leave as well. This part is still very much up in the air.

> When Genevieve Stevens defected from the Stevens Empire, she officially adopted the name "Jonny-V", because that was the name she answered to from the only person who ever respected her. Now that she was no longer a part of the Stevens Empire, people around her no longer treated her like a pariah. However, the damage to her goodwill had already been done by the abuse from her father and his peers. Because she was no longer under the empire's restrictive heel, Jonny-V could pursue her lifelong dream to become a mage, and wasted no time in doing so. Because of her hard work and fascination for the art of magic, it wasn't long before she was granted a bit of magic energy of her own. (I'm not yet sure what magic order Jonny-V joins, but it will most likely be controlled by a neutral deity, and contains an variety of very destructive spells.) Her ultimate goal soon became to train herself into an unstoppable fighter, and to someday single-handedly bring down her father's empire, once and for all avenging everything he and his acquaintances did to her.

> Right after she became a mage, Jonny-V began traveling throughout Nnet Olotor as a drifter, constantly practicing her combat and magic skills, and looking for new ways to test her limits. Unfortunately, this practicing is often done at the expense of anyone who gets in her way or confronts her. As Jonny-V's past abuse by her father and his acquaintances diminished her innocence, she built an emotional wall around herself in order to cope with it. At the time, she was afraid to talk back to them, because she knew what they were capable of, so she was forced to keep her emotions bottled up. Jonny-V is constantly haunted by the fact she didn't retaliate against them when she had a chance, but does so vicariously through other people with her violent and explosive personality. She picks a fight at every chance she gets, and never, ever backs down from one. She takes every opportunity she can to oppose powerful enemies and increase her fighting skills. She wins fights more often than she loses them, and becomes more powerful whatever the outcome. As Jonny-V's power increased, so did her boldness. She began traveling to places where she knows security is tight, and then causing trouble in order to draw the attention of the law enforcers. She then engages the police in battle, and usually ends up killing or incapacitating all who try to stop her. But after being nearly killed in battle on a few occasions, Jonny-V has become more selective in her battles, only choosing fights she feels she can win. Jonny-V has developed a thirst for mortal combat and takes great pleasure in killing her opponents. However, if an opponent puts up a sufficiently good fight, Jonny-V may choose to spare him/her so that he/she can give Jonny-V another good fight in the future.

> Despite Jonny-V's evil nature and apparent lack of conscience, she does have her own code of ethics that she doesn't expect anyone else to understand. She considers the only acceptable means to die is in battle at the hands of a superior opponent. She considers herself to be a courageous warrior, and despises cowardice. As brutal and bloodthirsty as she is, she would never sneak attack her enemies, and only kills someone after engaging them in combat. Jonny-V secretly wants to return to her nice and trusting self, but doesn't know if she'll ever be able to work up the courage to do so, despite her boldness in combat. She refuses to be nice to anyone in fear of someone taking advantage of her goodwill, like those during her childhood did. Because her trust was repeatedly trampled on during her days in the Empire, she has vowed to never trust anyone again. Besides, she figures that she's already made too bad of a reputation for herself for anyone to be friends with her anyway.

> Jonny-V is currently an above average fighter and an exceptionally powerful mage for someone who has been in the practice for less that four years. She hasn't learned a lot of spells in her magic order, but she has nearly mastered the few she does know, and makes them very powerful. She has also learned a few magic techniques, some of which she developed herself. Jonny-V takes her combat and magic skills very seriously, because they are all she ever trusts. She knows she will most likely confront her father or his empire at some point in time, and she's willing to do whatever it takes to prepare herself for such a battle. Besides gaining power through combat experience, she occasionally searches for ways to learn new spells in her magic order, usually by reading literature about her magic order, and vigorously practicing any new spells she learns. I may even have her enter some kind of fighting tournament, in order to test herself against some of the world's best fighters. Jonny-V is proficient in small blades, such as daggers and throwing knives, and also in long polearms. Her weapon of choice is a lightly enchanted staff, which has a foot-long blade at one end, and possibly an magic gem just behind the blade. I'm not yet sure how Jonny-V acquires such a weapon, but it will most likely be a prize won from a defeated enemy.

> The story begins with Jonny-V adventuring on her own, before she met Oinkillune and Binky. At beginning of the story, I will try to focus on Jonny-V's personality, give hints as to why she's always pissed off, and how she came to be that way. I will also focus on her level of fighting skills, reveal a few of her attacks, and her ferocity in battle. When Jonny-V finally does meet Oinkillune, she will inadvertantly or reluctantly save his life, that of Binky's, or both. One of the gwahrais' cultural laws states that if anyone saves the life of a gwahrai, that gwahrai becomes obligated to accompany his/her rescuer until he/she returns the deed. Jonny-V is vehimently opposed to the two joining her, because she feels that they would be a liability in her quest to overthrow her father's empire. Oinkillune is equally opposed to the arrangement, because he is extremely uncomfortable with Jonny-V's violent and hostile nature. However, he agrees to leave the forest for the first time in his life in order to honor is adopted people's traditions. Jonny-V reluctantly agrees with letting Oinkillune and Binky tag along, but threatens the two with bodily harm if they ever get in her way. Because it is against her code of ethics to just kill the two, she tries other means of getting them off her back throughout the next few chapters, all without success. However, once Jonny-V sees Oinkillune in battle, she is amazed at his skills, and immediately figures out that he's a Zodian angel. Realizing that Oinkillune has a fighting potential that far exceeds that of her own, Jonny-V gains a newfound respect for the young gwahrai elf, and accepts him as her ally.
