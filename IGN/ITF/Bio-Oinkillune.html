Oinkillune

 - CHARACTER STATS -
Real Name - Oinkillune
Race - Angelic Elven
Residence - The Gwahrai Forest
Demeanor - Courageous, selfless, heroic, but overly trusting and naive
Age - 27 (approx. 14 in human years)
Height/Weight - 5' 1" / 120lbs
Languages - Gwahrai (fluent), primary elven language

> Oinkillune is a young teenage elf who was abandoned by his mother as an infant. He was adopted by a gwahrai couple from his infancy, and lives with them in their home in a village in the woods. He is someone with a high degree of ethical standards, and puts the needs of others ahead of his own, often going out of his way to help total strangers in need without expecting anything in return. Despite being drastically physically different from his gwahrai peers, he has no trouble fitting into their society, and has become an excellent tree climber despite not having a tail or hands for feet. He values the well being of his family and community over all things. Oinkillune has a gentleman-like and magnetic personality, tends to get along with everyone he meets, and never looks down upon people.

> Oinkillune considers his biggest challenge to be looking after his 13 year old (approximately nine in human years) sister Binky. Binky is exceptionally curious, even by gwahrai standards, and does not know the difference between an act of bravery and a foolish risk. She is extremely reckless and must be watched at all times in order to be kept out of harm's way. Binky's reckless actions have included things like challenging predatory beasts to one-on-one fights, and attempting dangerous stunts, but her brother has always been fortunate enough to consistently save Binky from her own suicidal nature.

> Binky is not Oinkillune's only concern in life. Although the gwahrai who raised Oinkillune are a peaceful community, they are no strangers to violence. They constantly live under the threat of not only attacks from the predatory beasts that regularly try to feed on them, but also from the occasional invasion from rogue states. Like most of the members of his adopted race, Oinkillune carries a gwahrai thornstick and sling with him at all times, and is always ready to join his community in defending the village should such an emergency arise. However, despite bearing the responsibility of defending his community, Oinkillune always doubted if he could ever bring himself to use his weapons for lethal force if he needed to. He is someone who shuns violence, preferring to reason with his enemies, and only uses violence when absolutely necessary.

> Since Oinkillune's birth, he's never left the forest he has always called home. As the result, he's never gotten know much about any of Nnet Olotor's races other the gwahrai who raised him, and their elven neighbors who's homes are scattered throughout the forest floor. However, something happened to his community that would have a lasting impact on the way he felt about the human race. A few years ago, the Stevens Empire began expanding their army. Because the interdimensional portal had recently been shut down by Nnet Olotor's good armies, the empire needed vast quantities of building materials to maintain and expand their army. One of these materials was wood, and in order to obtain the amount they needed, the empire began to clear cut the forest the gwahrai depended on for survival. When the gwahrai realized the empire's clear cutting campaign was headed towards their villages, they attacked the empire, driving them away from the forest. However, the Stevens Empire soon returned with a vengeance. Several hundred imperial troops stormed the forest, killing everything in sight, and laying torch to the trees in an effort to burn the gwahrai from their hiding places. With the help of their elven allies, and perhaps even a bit of divine intervention, the gwahrai were able to defeat the invaders, but the price of victory was high. Nearly half of the gwahrai homes were destroyed, and several hundred gwahrai and elves perished, including some of Oinkillune's closest friends. As a result of the attack, Oinkillune developed a deep hatred towards the human race. Because he had never seen or heard of any humans other than the ones who attacked his village, it was easy for him to assume that all humans were inherently heartless and bloodthirsty savages.

> When Oinkillune was 12 years old, he discovered a strange energy coursing through his body. At first, neither he nor his gwahrai family knew what the energy was all about; some thought there was something wrong with Oinkillune, while others thought it was some kind of a divine sign. However, when one of the elves, who lived on the forest floor and frequently traveled outside the forest, heard of Oinkillune's energy, he told the young elf that he was a Zodian angel, and that the energy within him was magic energy. He then went on to tell Oinkillune everything he knew about Zod and the Zodian angels, and that Oinkillune should never let anyone know that he was such an angel. If the wrong people were to know what he was, they would hunt him down and kill him. If ever asked about his magic energy, the traveling elf suggested that Oinkillune pass himself off as a standard mage. Oinkillune was devastated to learn that he was spawned by a being of such evil for the purpose of evil, and vowed never to use his magic energy in fear of supporting the deity. However, after the attack on his home by the Stevens Empire, Oinkillune began to use his magic energy to enhance the fighting abilities he'd already learned from his family. This made him a far more powerful fighter than before, but although he began integrating magic techniques into his fighting skills, he still refused to learn any of the actual spells in the magic order he was bound to. Oinkillune is naturally an elf, and was taught to fight with gwahrai techniques, and this added to his magic energy gives him a fighting potential that far surpasses that of a normal gwahrai or elf, even without the knowledge of Zodian spells. He is so fast and agile, that he can single handedly take down a pair of predatory beasts, or a small group of highly trained and well armed Stevens imperial soldiers without taking a single hit.

> Fortunately for Oinkillune, his family did not treat him as a pariah upon learning about the evil energy that flowed within him. They instead decided to judge him by his good nature, and most of them were glad that he had the energy to assist him in fighting off predators, and even more so rouge states, mainly the Stevens Empire. In fact in the years to come, the empire attacked the gwahrai villages again to clear the way for chopping down their forest for wood. But when the empire learned of the elf who they considered to be a god-like warrior, most of their troops refused to return to the forest in fear of facing him. As a result, the empire eventually stopped attacking the forest in favor of easier targets. But despite the tremendous power that Zod's magic energy gives him, he sees this energy as a curse and a disease, and has always had nightmares about it consuming his soul, and turning him into a monster that would commit unimaginable acts of evil. Oinkillune hopes to someday find a way to purge Zod's magic energy from his body, and to earn the magic energy of a deity whose ideals more closely match his own.

> Oinkillune enters the story when he meets Jonny-V in the forest. (I'm not yet sure why Jonny-V chose to enter the Gwahrai Forest. Maybe she picked a fight with the wrong people and had to flee after she realized she was hopelessly outmatched, and that her escape route just happened to lead to Oinkillune's village. Maybe Jonny-V heard the rumors about an ultra-powerful fighter who protected the woods from outsiders, and went there specifically to challenge Oinkillune to a fight. Or maybe she was passing through. At this point, I just haven't figured it out yet.) She accidentally or reluctantly saves Oinkillune's or Binky's life, forcing Oinkillune to honor a gwahrai custom in which if someone saves the life of a gwahrai, that gwahrai must accompany his/her savior until the favor is likewise returned. The thought of fulfilling this tradition makes Oinkillune cringe, because not only will he be venturing outside of the forest for the first in his life, but he will have to be in the constant presence of a human who perfectly fits the bloodthirsty and ruthless stereotype he's pinned on the entire human race. Binky also comes along for the adventure, although I haven't yet figured out exactly why. (She could have snuck out of the forest to accompany her brother, and to get a sense of what a real adventure is like. Or it could be because it was Binky's life who Jonny-V saved, making her the one bound to her people's custom, and Oinkillune decided to tag along to protect his sister.)

> When Oinkillune and Binky first begin adventuring with Jonny-V, the human mage sees her two new followers as useless baggage and threatens to physically harm them unless they stay out of her way. She is rude and hostile towards them, but Oinkillune soon discovers that Jonny-V was once potentially a good and caring person until her father's abuse changed all that. Despite Jonny-V's rudeness and violent nature, and the way Oinkillune feels about the human race, he instinctively tries to see whatever good is left in her. He feels that Jonny-V wants to go back to her good self, and helps her do so, even though Jonny-V had long given up on that goal. But in helping Jonny-V return to her good self, Oinkillune learns that not all humans are monsters, and not to judge people simply because of their race. When Jonny-V learns that Oinkillune is a Zodian angel, and that he refuses to learn the spells in his magic order, she becomes enraged and accuses the young elf of making himself selectively weak for the sake of worthless ethics. Even so, she recognizes Oinkillune as a powerful ally, and eventually respects him for his combat skills. With him at her side, Jonny-V would now have a far greater chance of achieving her ultimate goal of overthrowing her father's empire.
