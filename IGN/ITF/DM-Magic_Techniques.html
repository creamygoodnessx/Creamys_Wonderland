Magic Techniques

> Magic techniques are basic manipulations of a mage's magic energy. They are easy to learn, extremely useful, and mages usually experiment with them before learning the spells of their magic orders. These techniques are easy to learn primarily because unlike spells, they require no specific thought patterns or spoken words to perform, and require only the most basic of body motions, but they still take a certain amount of mental focus to pull off. The main drawback of magic techniques is that they usually drain magic energy faster than spells do, since they involve the direct use of this energy, as opposed to converting it into other forms the way spells do. Most of these techniques involve the mage projecting and reshaping his/her magic energy in some way, while others involve moving it from one point to another.

MAGIC BOLT - Quite simply, a projectile made of pure, condensed magic energy, ejected at a high rate of speed from the mage's body. Some mages tether a dense ball of energy by a magic thread attached to one of their fingers , and then spin it around their hand at a high rate of speed, finally launching it at a target by mentally severing the thread, much like using a sling to launch a stone. Other mages allow energy to build on one of their shoulders, allowing the energy to become increasingly dense, and then firing it by letting it travel along their arm and out of their finger. Magic bolts can hit with the force ranging from that of a sling stone or of a cannonball or stronger, depending on the skill of the mage, and the amount of magic energy invested in the technique.

MAGIC ROPE - A long thread of magic energy tethered to the mage's body. This technique has a variety of uses, including acting as a climbing rope, a whip, or a lariat. The strength of the magic rope depends on the amount of magic energy invested in the technique, and how well the mage can mentally focus on keeping the rope intact. Because the rope never leaves the mage's body, no magic energy is depleted from the mage unless a portion of the rope is severed from the mage's body.

MAGIC SHIELD - A wall of condensed magic energy, projected in front of the mage. The mage can invest additional energy into the shield to either make it thicker to block incoming attacks and hostile spells more effectively, or to make it wider to cover a larger area. If a strong enough attack strikes the shield, it may shatter the shield, and drain the mage of the energy the mage invested in creating it.

MAGIC BUBBLE - A protective, airtight bubble of magic energy which completely surrounds the mage. Like the magic shield, it protects the mage against incoming hostile attacks. The bubble protects the mage from every side, but is usually thinner and weaker, and thus less effective than the shield. Investing additional energy into the bubble will make the shield either thicker and stronger, or larger, covering a wider area. Mages using this technique must be careful to not let the bubble run out of air, and dismantle it before that happens.

ENCHANT WEAPON - The ability to temporarily enchant a melee weapon with magic energy, making it stronger and more effective in combat. Enchanted weapons and shields can be used to block powerful attacks more effectively, but blocking attacks this way is usually less effective than with the magic shield or magic bubble. An enchanted weapon can also be thrown at enemies while a thread of magic energy tethers it to the user, and then pulled back into the mage's hand. The enchantment only lasts as long as the weapon is magically connected to the mage.

LIFE TO MAGIC - The ability of a mage to convert his/her own life energy into magic energy. The more life energy the mage converts, the more exhausted he/she will likely feel afterwards. The mage must be careful not to drain his/her life energy too far, or he/she will be too tired to fight, and in some rare cases, may even die of exhaustion. This technique is usually used only as a last resort if the mage needs the additional energy in a life or death situation.

DONATE MAGIC - The ability of a mage to donate a portion of his/her magic energy to another, friendly mage. This technique will not work unless both mages belong to the same magic order.

MAGIC LINK - The ability of several mages to temporarily share their magic energy between their bodies. The mages stand close together while a thread of magic energy connects each of the mages together, like mountain climbers connected by a rope. This technique is used primarily when several mages cast a powerful spell that takes up more energy that a single mage can hold in his/her body at once, or when a spell or another technique needs to be exceptionally powerful. Magic Link will only work if all of the mages performing it belong to the same magic order.
