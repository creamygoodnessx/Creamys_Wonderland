Gwahrai

--- PHYSICAL DESCRIPTION ---
* The gwahrai are a small, tree-dwelling race found almost exclusively in one particular large forest on Nnet Olotor. They are named by the elves after their battle cry / distress call, which sounds like a high-pitched "gwaaahhhh!". The gwahrai are naturally equipped for the forest they call home. They have small and slender lemur-like bodies, small heads, long bushy tails, an extra pair of hands in place of feet, and usually stand on all-fours.
* the average adult gwahrai stands 90 centimeters tall, and weighs around 20 pounds.

--- CIVILIZATION ---
* because members of both genders are physically very similiar, females are seen as social equals to their male counterparts.
* because the forest provides the gwahrai everything they need for survival, and it's warm all year long, the gwahrai are able to establish permanent villiages, and don't have to migrate.
* frequently seen in small groups venturing outside the forest, often with the company of woodland elves.
* Although a rare occurence, some gwahrai families have been known to live in the civiliations of elves and humans. However, the gwahrai civilization does not expand and settle into new territories, but stick to the forest generation after generation.
* The benefits and hazards to a gwahrai's lifespan have helped the gwahrai to establish a population equilibrium with their environment, so that they neither have to worry about overpopulation nor endangerment of extinction.
* overwhelmingly peaceful, but will not hesitate to defend themselves when need be.
* Some gwahrai villiages are shared peacefully with woodland elves in what can be called a "bunk-bed" coexsistance; the elves living on the forest floor while the gwahrai reside high in the trees. The two races intermingle in certain events, such as out-of-forest adventures, hunting trips, survival training, and festivals.
* The gwahrai were the primary influence of the culture of the first generations of wood elves, and shaped how the elves felt about things like gender equality, law, and warfare.
* Can't understand why the other races are so materialistic, and see materialism in general as a weakness.
* Greatly value individual freedom
* Have an extraordinary knowledge of the forest. (What berries are good to eat, how to make crude weapons and tools, etc.)
* Since enough food grows wild in the forest to support the gwarai population, they never needed to develop agriculture.

--- COMBAT ---
* Because the gwahrai share their forest home with predators that constantly hunt them, the gwahrai have been forced to develop certain defensive methods.
* fight in small groups
* The gwahrai do not like fighting out in the open, and since they are almost always significantly smaller than their enemies, they obviously can't win by brute force. Instead, they use their environment to their advantage, attacking the enemy with blowguns and slings while remaining hidden. They like to attack in this manner until they have driven off or killed the enemy, or until discovered. If the latter occurrs, the gwahrai then pounce on the enemy in relatively large numbers, and attack with thornsticks and foriegn weapons they obtained through trade.
* The prefered weapon of the gwahrai is the gwahrai thornstick. The thornstick is a 50 centimeter long club made of the flexible, yet amazingly durable, wood from their native trees. At the end of the thornstick is a razor-sharp thorn, which adds an additional 15 to 25 centimeters to the club.
* The gwahrai also use small weapons manufactured by other races. These weapons are most commonly daggers, but also include short swords (a long sword to a gwahrai), spike-knuckles, and ... A fighting gwahrai depends on his/her quickness and agility to win a battle, and shuns anything that compromises it, such as heavy armor, and heavy or any two-handed weapon.
* There are no specially trained or professional soldiers. Rather than having a society that depends on an army or police force to protect them, everyone, from (the lowest to highest members of society), and of both genders, is trained in the art of combat from an early age. In the event of a predator attack or an invasion, the civilians can postpone their daily duties and go into their "combat mode" at the drop of a hat, in order to come to the defense of their villiage.
* When fighting along side elves, the contrasting fighting techniques of the elves and gwahrai complement each other well, making them a very formidable fighting force.
* Fighting gwahrai rely on their great quickness and agility to bring down enemies, and shuns anything that interferes with these qualities. When forced to fight in close quarters, they try to always be ready to dodge their enemies attacks, and immediately launch a counter-attack. Since the gwahrai are used to moving so quickly and fluidly through the trees, swinging on vines, bouncing off and climbing of the sides of trees, and using both pairs of hands and their tails to grab and hang from branches and vines, heavy armor and any two-handed objects would seriously hinder their movements, thus making them far less formidable in battle.
