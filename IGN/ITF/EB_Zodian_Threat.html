Evil Blood: The Zodian Threat

> Twenty-eight years ago, one of Nnet Olotor's bloodiest and longest lasting wars came to an end. Over the centuries, worshippers of the evil deity Zod were slowly growing in number, and were notorious for their brutal and savage ways, including their need to sacrifice people and animals in order to appease their god. Nnet Olotor's good armies never saw the Zodians as a major threat to the world, because they believed that they were well prepared for, and could easily handle anything the Zodians threw at them, but their confidences were soon shattered. When Zod's army was powerful enough, and his religious community large enough to make his magic order a formidable enough force, he unleashed a devastating attack on the good armies of Nnet Olotor, marking the beginning of the Zodian War, the biggest war in the world's history. I haven't yet established too many details about the war yet, and at this point, most of it is still up in the air. By the time the war was over, the good armies had suffered heavy losses, but a great majority (possibly 90 percent of more) of Zod's worshippers had been killed, severely crippling him and his magic order.

> According to legend, when defeat for the Zodians became obvious, and while Zod still had enough magic energy to do so, he used up the last of it to magically impregnate many thousands of elven women throughout Nnet Olotor, resulting in the birth of the Zodian angels, while sacrificing himself in the process. The legend states that any one of these inherently evil angels could resurrect his/her creator with the help of inborn information on how to do so. However, no one really knew for sure exactly what the process of the resurrection was, or how to prevent it. So, the public turned to a magic artifact known as the Orb of Knowledge for answers. The Orb was the people's direct link to the deity of knowledge who collected information from the thoughts of every sentient being on Nnet Olotor, including those who did not worship him, and freely distributed that information to anyone who touched the Orb. But not even the deity of knowledge was able to give any definite answers about Zod's resurrection, only the theories that people had come up with over the years. The public began to grow increasingly desperate for a solution, and the people who were responsible for Zod's demise swore to prevent the evil deity from rising again, whatever the cost. They began to hunt down and kill anyone who was suspected of being an angel of Zod, as well as anyone who tried to defend them. It's one of those things where, over time, good becomes evil and evil becomes good. The very people who saved the world from Zod's genocide were now committing genocide themselves, and the victims were innocent people who's only crime was sharing Zod's blood.
