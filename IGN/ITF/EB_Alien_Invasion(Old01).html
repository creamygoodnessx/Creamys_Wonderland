Evil Blood: Alien Invasion
(4/28/2001 - 8/12/2002)

> The story of Evil Blood is set in a dimension that is parallel to Earth, during Earth's Crusades or some similar time period. This alternate dimension is currently called Nnet Olotor, although the name of this universe is very likely to change. Unlike Earth, Nnet Olotor has a strange energy field that enables intelligent beings to cast magic. It is a world that is inhabited by several races of people, but until 20 years ago, bore no humans. These first humans were pulled from Earth in some kind of freak accident, either a teleportation spell gone awry, or by a natural interdimensional phenomenon. Either way, the new humans convinced the Nnet Olotorians to open a permanent gateway, so that the people of both worlds could trade freely. This gesture of courtesy of the Nnet Olotorians was to be their potential downfall, and would change their way of life forever. Not long after the permanent gateway was opened, the humans began to show their true nature. They began pillaging Nnet Olotorian civilizations, converting the Nnet Olotorians to their religions, enslaving them, and committing genocide. The Nnet Olotorians knew the Earthlings were not going to stop until they've taken everything the Nnet Olotorians had worked so hard to gain. In desperation, they closed the gate connecting the two worlds, stopping the flood of reinforcements from the Earth, but by then it was too late. The humans had already mastered the art of magic, and were over 30,000 strong on Nnet Olotor. However, they did not know how to open another gate back to Earth, and this brought the war to a stalemate. They did however, come to understand that only a select few Nnet Olotorian wizards knew how to open another interdimensional portal, and shared the secret with an intelligent magical item they called the Orb of Knowledge. The humans knew that without reinforcements from Earth, they would be vulnerable to any Nnet Olotorian counter-attack. The humans' only hope to conquer Nnet Olotor was to extract the information they needed on opening a new gate to Earth from the Orb. However, even though the Orb was openly accessible to the public and shared its secrets with anyone who touched it, it would always deny certain knowledge to anyone with evil intentions, and to anyone who tried to extract sensitive information on behalf of such people. But that wouldn't stop the humans from trying, and the Nnet Olotorians knew that unless the humans were brought under control soon, they would be successful in opening another portal to Earth, and all would be lost for Nnet Olotor.
