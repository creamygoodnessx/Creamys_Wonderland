Evil Blood: Alien Invasion

> The story of Evil Blood is set on Nnet Olotor, sometime after Earth's fall of its Roman Empire, but before its Scientific Revolution. Nnet Olotor is a dimension chronologically parallel to Earth, and is inhabited by a small number of races of sentient beings, but until about 20 years ago, bore no humans. These first humans were pulled from Earth in some kind of freak accident, either a teleportation spell gone awry, or by a natural interdimensional phenomenon. Either way, the new humans convinced the Nnet Olotorians to open a permanent gateway, so that the people of both worlds could trade freely. This gesture of courtesy of the Nnet Olotorians was to be their potential downfall, and would change their way of life forever.

> Not long after the permanent gateway was opened, a human aristocrat by the name of Alex Stevens, obsessed with the extreme wealth of the Nnet Olotorian kingdoms, began a campaign to conquer these kingdoms, steal whatever he could get his hands on, and escape to the safety of Earth. He assembled a small army of about 70 or 80 human men, and somehow managed to overthrow a major Nnet Olotorian city state. I'm not yet exactly sure how Stevens manages to put his army together, but I am considering a number of possibilities. Stevens is a devout Christian, so he will definitely use that to influence others in his faith to assist him. Stevens will use this influence to assemble his first army, to expand his army after he conquers the city state. He may even claim that he was visited by the Christian god himself, and asked to purge Nnet Olotor of its "ungodly infidels" or something along those lines. This wouldn't be too hard for him to do so, because of the extreme moral differences between people of his faith, and most Nnet Olotorian cultures. Stevens or his family may also have ties with the military, and he may be a high ranking officer himself with a few forces of his own. His first army could even be a private army, or perhaps even hired thugs. As for how Stevens overthrows the Nnet Olotorian city state, the most likely scenario I'll go with is Stevens and his small army posing as friendly visitors to the city, then taking the city's leaders by surprise and holding them hostage, much like the Spanish conquistador Francisco Pizarro did to the Incan leader.

> After Stevens captured the Nnet Olotorian city state, he expanded his army significantly and declared war on Nnet Olotor as well as anyone who stood in the way of his interests. His army then began conquering other Nnet Olotorian civilizations, converting the Nnet Olotorians to Christianity, raping elven women, enslaving people, committing genocide, and perhaps they even went as far as to nearly wipe out an entire race of people or religious faith. Along the way, Stevens even had the nerve to proclaim himself 'Emperor'. Conquering Nnet Olotorian civilizations would not be easy for the Emperor. Although his army was growing steadily, it wouldn't be enough for him to pull off any invasions against any major Nnet Olotorian strongholds without some kind of distinct advantage. The Emperor's army would be at a monumental disadvantage, because he would have no spellcasters within his ranks. This is because Nnet Olotorian magic is a divine power, controlled and governed by Nnet Olotorian deities, and thus, gaining the ability to cast spells requires faith in a Nnet Olotorian deity. Emperor Stevens' army is strictly Christian, and he considers following any other religion to be heresy. However, he may be able to convince rogue Nnet Olotorian nations who do practice magic to assist him, leveling the playing field. It's even possible that Stevens brought some things from Earth that were alien to the Nnet Olotorians, such as horses, certain military tactics, or weapons, and was somehow able to use these to his advantage. In order for Stevens to bring anything from Earth to Nnet Olotor, he would have to seize control of the interdimensional portal. At some point of his invasion, he takes the gate by force, then builds his heavily fortified capitol city around it. I'm not yet sure if the gateway is in the first city Stevens conquered, or if he took control of it at some later point of his reign of terror. Not all humans supported Emperor Stevens. Most of the humans on Nnet Olotor were friendly with the world's natives, and grew increasingly uncomfortable with the Emperor's aggressive tactics. Eventually, they began to actively and openly oppose the Emperor, and soon the human armies joined the war and allied themselves with those of Nnet Olotor. Not surprisingly, Emperor Stevens labeled the humans who opposed him and his interests to be 'race traitors', and vowed to deal with them appropriately.

> After Emperor Stevens conquered a small number of civilizations, maybe five or six, he had enough wealth to flee to Earth and live any way he wanted for the rest of his life. However, Stevens, blinded by greed, insisted on pushing further into Nnet Olotor, and would not be satisfied until he had taken every last item of value he could get his hands on. This greed proved to be his downfall, because it influenced the Nnet Olotorian forces to unleash a desperate, ultra-powerful attack on Stevens' capitol city, an attack they had vowed never to use unless as an absolute last resort, the same way a nuclear superpower today might feel about resorting to a nukes. I'm not sure exactly what this attack will be. It could be the one, ultimate desperation spell of a deity's magic order, it could be a massive swarm of giant, flesh-eating insects, or it could even be some kind of divine intervention. Whatever the attack, it will take the Stevens Empire completely by surprise, leave its capitol city in a smoldering ruin, and destroy the interdimensional portal, not only severing the Empire's only supply route from Earth, but preventing Emperor Stevens from escaping as well. Stevens himself will survive the attack, either because he was warned in advance to leave the city, or simply wasn't there at the time.

> Although the attack devastated the Stevens Empire, they were still a significant threat to Nnet Olotor. The Empire was still recruiting any humans they could lure in by taking advantage of their religious beliefs, and by promising them a piece of whatever loot they plundered. The Empire also continued to form new alliances with rogue armies by helping them in their interests. Soon, the Empire and their allies were soon considered evenly matched with the good forces of Nnet Olotor. Of course, the Emperor himself had to remain in hiding from the hundreds of thousands of enemies he made during his invasions, but nevertheless, he was still able to remain in control of his forces. Some time before or during their crusade, the Stevens Empire had learned about the existence of an enchanted artifact known as the Orb of Knowledge. The Orb enabled people to communicate directly with Nnet Olotor's god of knowledge and secrets, who could read people's thoughts wherever his magic energy existed, and was the only deity on Nnet Olotor who could obtain information in this manner. If anyone in the world ever had any knowledge about how to open another gateway to Earth, the deity would have this knowledge as well. The Orb was kept in the deity's main temple, was openly accessible to the public, and shared its secrets with anyone who touched it. However, since the deity of knowledge was of good alignment, he would always deny certain knowledge to anyone with evil intentions, and to anyone who tried to extract sensitive information on behalf of such people. This would mean that the Orb would be useless to the Stevens Empire, because the deity supported the good armies of Nnet Olotor. But Emperor Stevens wasn't so easily discouraged. He knew that the Orb was his key to victory, and if he could somehow get the information he needed from the deity, he could open another portal to Earth, and get a second chance at fulfilling his dream of completely conquering Nnet Olotor.
