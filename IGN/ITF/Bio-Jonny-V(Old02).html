Jonny-V
(11/11/2003 to 10/12/2004)

 - CHARACTER STATS -
Real Name - Genevieve Alexandria Stevens
Race - Human
Residence - None: drifter
Demeanor - Hostile, stubborn, bitter, vengeful, but hard-working and courageous
Age - 20
Height/Weight - 5' 4" / 170lbs
Alignment - Chaotic Evil

> Genevieve Alexandria Stevens is the daughter of Earthen conqueror Emperor Stevens. She once had the potential of being a nice and sociable person, but an unkind childhood changed all that. Genevieve grew up in the Stevens Empire, a society dominated by idealism, in which its members are judged primarily by their social class, gender, and physical appearance. When Genevieve reached her adolescence, she did not fit the empire's ideals as a good looking young lady, and was given a very hard time for it from most of the people she knew, mainly her father and his elitist peers. (The ideal look for a woman in the Steven's Empire is to have a tall and slender body, and a youthful appearance, but Genevieve was a very heavy set girl in her adolescence.)  The empire is also heavily male-oriented, in which men get to make all the important decisions and hold positions of power, while women are meant to be seen and not heard, and are given far fewer rights than male counterparts. Genevieve refused to accept herself an an inferior, and always questioned the empire's gender biased views. She repeatedly did things that were only acceptable for men to do, such as carrying a weapon, and wearing certain male attire, which earned her further resentment. Because the Stevens Empire looked down on the practice of magic as witchcraft, Genevieve's open fascination with magic only made her even more of an outcast. Noone in Genevieve's childhood showed any compassion towards her, nor did they ever take anything she said or did seriously. Whenever she tried to speak her mind in a serious manner, it was always passed off as a joke. Her father didn't even care that she stood in the war room and listened in on his private military meetings with his generals, top military advisors, and his Zodian allies, and he never thought that Genevieve might someday turn on him. Genevieve spent her early years toeing the line for her father and peers, and did everything she could to please them, but all she ever got in return was disdain and ungratefulness. Whenever Genevieve tried to resolve a personal dispute with her father in a diplomatic and peaceful way, her efforts would always be thrown back in her face with hostility, and her father would remind her of how worthless she was. Every time such an incident occurred, it would stick into her memory for life, and permanently erode her ever diminishing good nature.

> Genevieve's only salvation came in the form of an elven slave who, like Genevieve, was an outcast to society, simply because of his race and social status. This slave idea is still relatively new, so I haven't yet come up with a name for him yet. Because the two were outcasts, it wasn't difficult for them to form a strong emotional bond. When Genevieve was twelve, she had once stood up for the slave, and protected him from a beating from his cruel owners. In return, the slave secretly spent his free time teaching her how to speak, read, and write his language as a sign of gratitude. Although the slave could speak English, he had a strong accent from his native language, which was Nnet Olotor's most common elven language. As a result, the slave kept mispronouncing Genevieve's name as "Johnny V". Genevieve took advantage of her new language abilities by reading elven literature and history. She also looked up everything she could about Nnet Olotor's various magic orders, and what she could do to someday become a mage herself. However, the Stevens Empire forbids the use of magic by law, and the penalty for violating this law is death. So, at the age of 16, Genevieve finally mustered up the courage to defect from the empire, to pursue her lifelong dream of becoming a mage. I'm not yet sure what happened to the slave. It's possible that Emperor Stevens brutally murdered him, because he discovered the truth and felt that the slave was corrupting his daughter, and that that was the final straw that pushed Genevieve to leave home. Or perhaps Genevieve saw a once in a lifetime opportunity to help him to escape the empire, and decided it was a good idea for her to leave as well. This part is still very much up in the air.

> When Genevieve Stevens defected from the Stevens Empire, she officially adopted the name "Jonny-V", since it was the name referred to her by the only person who ever appreciated her. Because she was no longer under the Stevens Empire's restrictive heel, Jonny-V could pursue her lifelong dream to become a mage, and wasted no time in doing so. Because of her hard work and fascination for the art of magic, it wasn't long before she was granted a bit of magic energy of her own. (I'm not yet sure what magic order Jonny-V joins, but it will most likely be controlled by a neutral deity, and contains an variety of very destructive spells.) Right after she became a mage, Jonny-V began traveling throughout Nnet Olotor as a drifter, constantly practicing her combat and magic skills, and looking for new ways to test her limits. She is a decent fighter and a relatively powerful mage for someone who has been in the practice for less that four years. She knows a good number of spells in her magic order, as well as a few magic techniques, some of which she developed herself. Jonny-V takes her combat and magic skills very seriously, because they are all she ever trusts. She knows she will most likely confront her father or his empire at some point in time, and she's willing to do whatever it takes to prepare herself for such a battle. I may even have her enter some kind of fighting tournament in order to test herself against some of the world's best fighters.

> After her defection, Jonny-V was finally starting to be respected as a person. The kindness showed to her by the citizens of Nnet Olotor was a welcome change from the abuse from her adolescence, but the emotional damage of her past had already taken its toll on her goodwill. As Jonny-V's past abuse by her father and his acquaintances diminished her innocence, she built an emotional wall around herself  in order to cope with it. At the time, she was afraid to talk back to them, because she knew what they were capable of, so she was forced to keep her emotions bottled up. Jonny-V is constantly haunted by the fact she didn't retaliate against them when she had a chance, but does so vicariously through other people with her violent and explosive personality. Jonny-V is hostile towards everybody, and refuses to be nice to anyone in fear of someone taking advantage of her goodwill, like those during her adolescence did. Because her trust was repeatedly trampled on during her days in the Empire, she has vowed to never trust anyone again. She secretly wants to return to her nice and trusting self, but doesn't know if she'll ever be able to work up the courage to do so. Besides, she figures that she's already made too bad of a reputation for herself for anyone to be friends with her anyway.

> At some point early on in the story, Jonny-V meets Oinkillune. Originally, I was going to have it so that Jonny-V had been watching Oinkillune's every move through a magic artifact she took from Zod's fortress before she defected from the empire. The artifact would have allowed Jonny-V to see the world through Oinkillune's eyes. After her defection, she would have visited Oinkillune and demand that he help her take down the Stevens Empire. However, after much thought, I've dropped this element from Evil Blood, and now I'm not really sure exactly how the two meet. The two will most likely have at least a few adventures together, although I only have a few sketchy ideas as to exactly what they may or may not go through. Jonny-V envies Oinkillune for growing up in a loving environment, and wonders if the world would survive his potential wrath if he had grown up the way she had. It also upsets Jonny-V that she had to work hard to build up every bit of her magic energy, while Oinkillune was simply born with his. As the story develops, certain events cause Jonny-V's negative traits to slowly recede, thanks in no small part to Oinkillune.
