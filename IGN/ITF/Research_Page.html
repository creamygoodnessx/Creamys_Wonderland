Research Notes

> Finishing my Evil Blood project requires a lot of research into various topics. This page is dedicated to books I've read and the TV documentaries I've studied in order to gain the knowledge I need to make this project count for something. One of the major cornerstones of the Evil Blood back-story is evolution. Most things in reality go through their own version of evolution, including the animal and plant kingdoms, languages, sports, and legal systems. Because I'm building Nnet Olotor from scratch, and will let the world evolve naturaly in my mind starting at the time of the first micro-organisms, having a deep understanding of how evolution works will be crucial. Somewhere down the line, I'm planning to start writing the Nnet Olotorian languages. As you may or may not know, my previous attempt at writing a conlang was nothing short of a disaster. If I can write a few really good languages for the Nnet Olotorian people, and make up for my previous failed attempt, that would be pretty cool. However, I'd like to research the subject of languages before I begin writing one. I plan on checking out two or more books from my local library on the origin and development of languages, and may even go as far as learning a second language, probably German, Japanese, or Arabic.

> Within the listed pages below are the bits of information that I found most interesting in the book or documentary I saw them in. Any green text you see is how a particular note may apply to Evil Blood.

Evidence of Evolution (1968) by Nicholas Hotton III - An outdated but interesting book on the subject of evolution.

Of Pandas and People (1993) by Percival Davis and Dean H. Kenyon- Just a little further study to broaden my knowledge about evolution. This one is far more up to date than EoE.
