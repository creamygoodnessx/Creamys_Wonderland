Stardancer
(Spoiler)

> Later on in the story, Stardancer touches the Orb of Knowledge, and finds out who and what she is. Word had spread to the Stevens Empire about the Plant Queen, and her willingness to do favors in exchange for new lifeforms. The empire desperately needed to extract the information from the Orb of Knowledge that would allow them to create another permanent portal to Earth, so their reinforcements could pass through. Emperor Stevens sent a few of his troops on a boat filled with various Earthen animals to visit the Plant Queen. The Queen was impressed by the animals she would be adding to her 'memory', and in exchange, granted the empire's request to help them retrieve the desired information from the Orb. So she created Stardancer, a person of almost pure good, and specifically engineered her mind to get around the Orb's 'firewalls' so she could find out how to create the portal. (I haven't decided exactly what the Plant Queen put in Stardancer's mind to allow her to fool the Orb. In fact, I'm not sure if knowing that is important.) The Queen instructed her visitors to escort Stardancer to the Orb, then after she retrieves the desired information, to take her back to the Queen to be absorbed. This would allow the Plant Queen to acquire all of Stardancer's memories, memories the Queen would then pass on to the Stevens Empire.

> Creating a being of nearly pure good would inevitably cause certain complications for a mission of this nature. After touching the Orb, it would be highly likely that Stardancer would refuse to cooperate with the empire, since doing so would spell the end of Nnet Olotor. The God of Knowledge might even make Stardancer aware of what she is, and unlock her abilities as an angel of the Plant Queen. If Stardancer used these abilities to turn on the empire and escape, they would probably be powerless to stop her. As a counter-measure, the Plant Queen created a few angels who were to subdue Stardancer if she were to resist.

> However, the empire's plan suffered disastrous failure. On their way back to the mainland, while the other Plant Queen angels and most of the ship's crew were asleep, Stardancer wandered up to the deck and somehow fell overboard. She later washed ashore where she was found by our heroes. When the ship's crew found out what happened to their precious cargo, they decided to proceed to temple where the Orb was housed, under the assumption that Stardancer would show up to discover her identity. They planned to wait until after Stardancer's session with the God of Knowledge to pounce on their victim, then take her back to her creator.

Possible Fate of Stardancer

> I'm not yet sure what will happen to Stardancer. Originally, I wanted to have her give herself willingly to the Plant Queen in order to save the life of her friends. I haven't ruled out that possibility just yet, but here's something else I'm considering. When the Plant Queen created Stardancer, she intended her to live for only about a week, which would have been just enough time for her to get to the Orb of Knowledge, retrieve the information she needed, and then be brought back to the Plant Queen. Stardancer was never meant to live out a full and normal life, and she only has a life span of a few months, just like any other one of the Plant Queen's angels.

> As Stardancer nears the end of her life, and her plant angel body begins to wilt, Jonny-V and Oinkillune start looking for ways to extend the life of their friend. Jonny-V knows of a certain spell that can change one lifeform into another type of lifeform. But she can't cast the spell, because it's not part of her magic order. However, she and Oinkillune know of a powerful mage who does know the spell. This mage owes the party a favor, because they helped him during a previous episode of Evil Blood. The mage says that he has never heard of anyone using the spell on an angelic being before, and doesn't know what effect it will have on Stardancer. The mage plans to use the spell to turn Stardancer into a true human, and if the spell is successful, Stardancer should lose all of her supernatural abilities, since she won't be a plant angel anymore. Also, she will be just as vulnerable as a true human, as her wounds will no longer instantly heal, and she will no longer be able to regenerate lost limbs. She will also age just as any normal human would, and she can be hurt and killed just like any normal human.

> The mage casts the spell on Stardancer, successfully transforming her into a true human, and she feels rejuvenated and healthy again. However, the spell has an unexpected effect on her. As expected, Stardancer has lost her shape-shifting and super-healing abilities, and she has become as fragile as a true human. But strangely enough, she hasn't lost her emotion-reading abilities. In fact, her telepathic abilities have actually improved. Not only can Stardancer still read emotions, but she also seems to have gained full mind-reading abilities, and the ability to communicate telepathically with others. At first, noone could explain her increase in telepathic abilities. But then, Jonny-V remembered that when the party had met Margo Angle's family on their way to the Orb of Knowledge, the family mentioned that Margo had full telepathic abilities, and wondered why Margo's (Stardancer's) abilities had been reduced to emotion-reading only. As it turns out, the Plant Queen modeled Stardancer after Margo down to the last physical detail. All plant angels have the ability to read emotions, but for some reason, their telepathic abilities can't go beyond that. Therefore, when Stardancer was created, she was an exact physical copy of Margo, but the only thing holding back her full telepathy was her plant angel body. Once Stardancer was turned into a true human, the loss of her plant angel body unlocked her full telepathic abilities.
