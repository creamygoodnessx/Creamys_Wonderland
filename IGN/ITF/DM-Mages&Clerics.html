Mages and Clerics

 Mages: Earning the privilege

> On Nnet Olotor, a mage is basically a sentient being who learns and uses magic spells. Before someone can learn any spells, he/she must believe in and worship a given deity, just like any other follower of that deity. Since magic energy is the fuel of all spellcasting, a mage's body must become receptive to this energy in order to cast any spells. No naturally created person is born with this ability. Magic energy is a divine force, produced and governed by the deities, and only they have the ability to bestow their magical energy upon their worshippers. But they don't just give out their power freely to anyone who asks for it. Any aspiring mage must go through a series of rigorous tests in order to prove himself/herself worthy of the right to use the deity's magic. These tests vary from religion to religion, sometimes from deity to deity. In some orders of magic, the series of tests is extremely dangerous, and failure could result in death. In other orders, the tests are not dangerous, but anyone who fails will never be allowed to retake them, or will be required to wait a number of months or years before trying again. Some religions call for tests that may be in the form of state-run annual tournaments, in which during the ceremony at the end of the tournament, the very deities of the religion appear in physical form and grant their magic energy to the winners. I haven't yet thought of what any of these tests actually are, but they will always be designed to test the subjects to see if they have what it takes to support the interests of their religion and/or specific deity. When a mage is granted magical energy, his/her body gains the ability to absorb the magic energy of his/her deity from Nnet Olotor's energy field, and to store a certain amount of this energy within his/her body. The mage is also given access to all of the spells in the deity's magic order, except for the ones reserved for clerics and the deity himself/herself. After receiving these magical abilities, the mage must continue to remain loyal to his/her deity, and fulfill the deity's religious requirements, such as daily prayer, sabbath, dietary restrictions, alignment restrictions, or even animal sacrifices, depending on the religion and deity. A mage who fails to meet these requirements may have his/her magical abilities revoked, but can usually regain them through sufficient atonement.

Learning and casting spells

> After earning a deity's magic energy, a mage will have access to most of the spells in the deity's magic order, but must learn each one on his/her own. Many spells require the mage to perform spoken words and body motions to activate it, but these alone are never enough to cast the spells. The heart of all spells is their individual thought patterns that must be performed along with the words and body motions. Since the thought pattern of a spell is difficult to interpret accurately from the explanations of written text or a teacher, the mage must often figure out the pattern by practicing the spell through trial and error. The first few successful castings are often weak, but the results improve as the mage continues to fine tune the casting of the spell by making better educated guesses at the precise words, body motions and thought patterns through repeated castings. Perfecting a spell can take a few days to several years depending on the spell, because every successful or partially successful casting will drain a mage of a certain amount of his/her magic energy, and once it's gone, the mage will have to wait until he/she has enough power to try the spell again. A spellcaster's body is constantly drawing in his/her deity's magic energy from Nnet Olotor's energy field, and once he/she has used up all of his/her magic energy, it will usually take about twelve hours to fully recharge, but this process goes faster if the mage is asleep, taking only about eight hours.

> When a mage first receives his/her magical abilities, he/she is given as much energy as his/her body can comfortably hold, and the maximum amount of energy he/she can hold at once depends on his/her physical condition. As the mage's body gets used to the energy through repeated spellcasting over the years, this maximum will slowly increase. Powerful spells can cost a mage more magic energy than his/her initial maximum, but once the maximum exceeds the cost of a spell, it will become open to the mage once he/she learns it. If a mage tries to cast a spell he/she does not have enough magic energy to cast, the spell will die out before it is completed (assuming the mage is able to perform it correctly), wasting the mage's energy. A higher maximum magic energy capacity also allows a mage to cast simple spells more often before having to recharge. A mage will always take roughly the same amount of time to recharge his/her magic energy from empty to full regardless of his/her maximum capacity, because the increase of power is proportionate to the mage's maximum. This means that the higher a mage's maximum magic energy capacity is, the faster his/her body will regenerate the energy.

> The more powerful a spell is, the more difficult it is to cast, the longer it takes to learn, and more magic energy it drains from the caster. The simplest spells of any magic order often require a simple body motion, one or two words if any, the thought of a single image, usually take about a second to cast, and cost only a small amount of magic power. These spells are handy when the mage has only seconds to react to an emergency, and can include spells that levitate the caster or another object for a few seconds. The most powerful spells in a magic order eat up so much magic energy, that it takes several mages to pool their magic energy together and cast it in unison, and they all must perform it to perfection. Such spells may exceed two minutes in casting time. These ultra powerful spells range from city leveling earthquakes to a mass resurrections.

> Some spells even require material components to cast. These components can be anything from a dash of salt, to a live insect, to a bit of sulfur, and must be used in accordance to the spell being cast. For example, a spell that protects an area from fire would require the mage to draw a big circle of ash on ground around the intended parameter. A spell that allows someone to climb walls and ceilings like a spider (or a Nnet Olotorian equivalent) would require the recipient of the spell to eat a live spider as the spell is being cast.

Multiple magic orders

> It's possible for a mage on Nnet Olotor to obtain the powers of more than one deity. As I mentioned earlier, a mage must remain loyal to his/her deity in order to keep the magic energy received from that deity. There is a wide variety of deities on Nnet Olotor, many of them with conflicting ideals, moral standards, and religious laws. Therefore, worshipping certain combinations of deities is not possible for a mage, since a certain means of homage to one deity (required for a mage to keep the magic energy of that deity) may be a forbidden act to another (punishable by the loss of the mage's magic energy of that deity). The great majority of mages who cast spells from multiple magic orders only follow deities within the same religion, since the chances of a conflict are minimal this way.

> If a mage wishes to become a member of more than one magic order, he/she will need to earn the magical energy from all magic orders he/she wishes to cast spells from. In such a case, the magic energy from each of his/her magic orders will be divided evenly within his/her body. Thus, if when the mage gets blessed with magic energy from his/her second magic order, he/she will lose half of his/her magic power from the first order in order to accommodate his/her second order. Likewise, if the mage obtains a third magic order, the amount of magic energy from the mage's first two orders will be reduced, so that the energy of each of his/her three orders will be an even third of his/her total magic energy.

> Some spells are shared by a number of magic orders which they are applicable to. For example, a volcano god would grant fire and earth-based spells to his believers, while a sun god would grant fire and life-based spells. Since both deities have fire-based spells in their magic orders, both of their magic orders could offer similar spells, such as those that allow spellcasters to throw fireballs, or those that grant the caster fire resistance. If an experienced mage wishes to cast fire-based spells, but his/her magic order doesn't support any, he/she can begin worshipping a second deity who runs a magic order that does. The obvious choice who be a deity whose magic order specializes in fire-based magic, but if that deity's religious requirements conflict with the first deity's restrictions, the two magic orders are incompatible, and therefore obtaining the magic energy of the second order will cost the mage the ability to cast spells from the first. Since a mage who's broken his/her back to gain access to his/her first magic order, then invested months, perhaps years, learning and perfecting its spells would likely be hell-bent against letting all that hard work go down the tubes, losing it is not an option. The alternative would be to find an alternate magic order that also contains fire-based spells, but does not conflict with the first. This alternate second order may not have as many, or as desirable, fire-based spells as the other, but at least it won't conflict with the mage's original order.

Clerics: The spiritual leaders

> Every religion on Nnet Olotor has its share of clerics. In any religious community, the role of a cleric is to teach the community the ways of their religion, and to provide them with moral guidance, and thus, clerics are often seen as a positive role models. More importantly, the cleric is a community's link to his/her religion's worshipped deities. Because of this link, clerics often hold high positions of power. Depending on the religion, a cleric can either appoint people in the community as their city leaders, military leaders, and arbiters, or they can hold any or all of these positions themselves. In some religious communities, clerical positions are hereditary, while in others, any extremely loyal and experienced mage can be appointed cleric by the religious higher-ups of the community, or by the deities themselves. A few communities may even offer suffrage to its worshippers, so the general populace can choose their next religious leaders. Religions with small, isolated tribes often have only one or two local clerics. The larger organized religions may have different ranks of clerics, with the lower ranked clerics each running a single temple, a middle-ranked cleric being in charge of all the temples in a city, and a few high ranking clerics who govern the entire religion, and who are second only to the deities themselves.

> Like a mage, a cleric will have access to magic energy, and the ability to cast spells. However, there are fundamental differences between these two types of spellcasters. Since a cleric usually has a much stronger relationship with the deities he/she worships, he/she usually is given greater access the to magic of the religion. They will be able to learn spells from the magic orders of all the deities of the religion, but will not be given access to any magic orders outside the religion, even if no religious conflicts exist between such orders. Clerics will also be able to learn special clerical spells that are denied to a standard mage. These special spells are usually very powerful, and in most cases, a cleric will have more than enough magic energy to cast them.

Angelic spellcasters

> On Nnet Olotor, an angel is defined as a person who is a direct and unnatural creation of a deity, and is indistinguishable from a naturally born member of a race of sentience. Angels are born with the magic energy of a highly experienced cleric, but their energy capacity can potentially grow to nearly that of a deity. An angel has full access to the magic order of the deity that created him/her. He/she can cast spells that are normally only open to the deity, and can single handedly cast spells which normally require the collective energy of several spellcasters. However, he/she has to learn each individual spell on his/her own, as if he/she was a normal person. Angels are forever bound to the magic order they are born under; they cannot gain the magic energy of other deities, and therefore can't learn spells from other magic orders.
