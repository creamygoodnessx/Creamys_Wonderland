The Evil Blood Glossary

> Here's a little glossary I started in order to give my readers a convenient means of identifying various elements of Evil Blood. It may not look like much now, but this glossary will grow as I create new terms and phrases for any new story elements I come up with through the development of Evil Blood.


A * C * M

A

Angel - A person who is a direct and unnatural creation of a deity. Some deities can create angels at will, usually by magically creating and implanting them as embryos within the wombs of female hosts. After that, the angelic embryo, before and after birth, develops exactly as if it were a normal member of the race of its host, and will always be indistinguishable from a naturally born member of that race.

> Angels are the same in every way as their chosen races, with one exception. An angel begins life with a substantial amount of magic energy, unlike non-angelic mages who have to earn the privilege of obtaining this energy. An angel has full access to the magic order of the deity that created him/her. He/she can cast spells that are normally only open to the deity, and can single handily cast spells which normally require the collective energy of several spellcasters. However, he/she has to learn each individual spell on his/her own, as if he/she was a normal person. Angels are forever bound to the magic order they are born under; they cannot gain the magic energy of other deities, and therefore can't learn spells from other magic orders. Additional differences between angels and non-angels may apply, depending on the angel's purpose of existence.

> Once an angel is created, the deity no longer has any direct control over him/her. The angel's gender, alignment, and other features that randomly appear in any non-angel will likewise randomly appear in the angel, unless certain features are specifically chosen by the deity. An angel's true heritage is usually revealed after he/she either accidentally discovers his/her magic energy, or if this energy is discovered by a spell of detection. Even after making this discovery, the angel still won't know what magic order he/she is bound to until he/she experiments with spells of the different  orders. In some rare cases, angels go through life and die without ever knowing their true identity. An angel who discovers that he/she is an angel may not necessarily choose to support the interests of his/her creator, especially if the angel is of a different alignment from that of his/her creator.

C

Cleric - Every religion on Nnet Olotor has its share of clerics. In any religious community, the role of a cleric is to teach the community the ways of their religion, and to provide them with moral guidance, and thus, clerics are often seen as a positive role models. More importantly, the cleric is a community's link to his/her religion's worshipped deities. Because of this link, clerics often hold high positions of power. Depending on the religion, a cleric can either appoint people in the community as their city leaders, military leaders, and arbiters, or they can hold any or all of these positions themselves. In some religious communities, clerical positions are hereditary, while in others, any extremely loyal and experienced mage can be appointed cleric by the religious higher-ups of the community, or by the deities themselves. A few communities even offer suffrage to its worshippers, so the general populace can choose their next religious leaders. Religions with small, isolated tribes often have only one or two local clerics. The larger organized religions may have different ranks of clerics, with the lower ranked clerics each running a single temple, a middle-ranked cleric being in charge of all the temples in a city, and a few high ranking clerics who govern the entire religion, and who are second only to the deities themselves.

> Like a mage, a cleric will have magic energy, and the ability to cast magic. However, there are fundamental differences between these two types of spellcasters. Since a cleric usually has a much stronger relationship with the deities he/she worships, he/she usually is given greater access the to magic of the religion. They will be able to learn spells from the magic orders of all the deities of the religion, but will not be given access to any magic orders outside the religion, even if no religious conflicts exist between such orders. Clerics will also be able to learn special clerical spells that are denied to a standard mage. These special spells are usually very powerful, and in most cases, a cleric will have more than enough magic energy to cast them.

M

Mage - A sentient being who learns and uses magic spells. Magic is a divine power, and before a mage can cast any spells, he/she must earn the ability to have his/her body absorb his/her deity's magic energy from Nnet Olotor's energy field. A mage is responsible for supporting the interests of the deity he/she worships, as well as serving his/her religion and its worshippers.

Magic energy - 20,000 years ago, as the earliest people of Nnet Olotor began forming their religous beliefs, a strange energy field of unknown origin was introduced to the world. This energy field was somehow able to reshape itself in the form of the many gods and godesses that the worlds' civilizations came to believe in. After their creation, the deities began emitting energy of their own, which spread to every part of the world, from the shortest blade of grass, to as far into the sky as the eye could see. Each of Nnet Olotor's deities controls and governs his/her own catagory of magic energy, known as a magic order. Magic energy resides within the bodies of a mages, and makes it possible for them to cast spells. The ability of a mage's body to take in magic energy is a privilege that must be earned through a series of tests sanctioned by the mage's religion. If the mage passes these tests, he/she is bestowed the magic energy of his/her deity by the deity himself/herself, often in person.

Magic order - A category of Nnet Olotor's magic energy, created and governed by a deity, in which privileged people can cast a pre-determined set of spells that are appropriate to that deity. The strength of a magic order depends on how many people believe in its deity, and how loyal they are to the deity and his/her interests. The stronger a magic order, the more powerful its spells will be when cast by its mages.
