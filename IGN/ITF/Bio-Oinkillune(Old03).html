Oinkillune
(8/30/2002 to 10/2/2004)

 - CHARACTER STATS -
Real Name - Oinkillune
Race - Angelic Elven
Residence - ?
Demeanor - Courageous, Selfless, Heroic
Age - 27 (approx. 14 in human years)
Height/Weight - 5' 1" / 105lbs
Alignment - Lawful Good

> Oinkillune is a young teenage elf who was abandoned by his mother as an infant. He was adopted by a gwahrai couple from his infancy, and lives with them in their home in a village in the woods. He is someone with a high degree of ethical standards, and puts the needs of others ahead of his own, often going out of his way to help total strangers in need without expecting anything in return. Despite being drastically physically different from his gwahrai peers, he has no trouble fitting into their society, and has become an excellent tree climber despite not having a tail or hands for feet. He values the well being of his family and community over all things. Oinkillune has a somewhat feminine personality, and because of his high voice, slender frame, and sometimes girly mannerisms, he's often mistaken by strangers for a girl. He has a gentleman-like and magnetic personality, tends to get along with everyone he meets, and never looks down upon people.

> Oinkillune considers his biggest challenge to be looking after his 13 year old (approximately nine in human years) sister Binky. Binky is exceptionally curious, even by gwahrai standards, and does not know the difference between an act of bravery and a foolish risk. She is extremely reckless and must be watched at all times in order to be kept out of harm's way. Binky's reckless actions have included things like challenging predatory beasts to one-on-one fights, and attempting dangerous stunts, but her brother has always been fortunate enough to consistently save Binky from her own suicidal nature.

> Binky is not Oinkillune's only concern in life. Although the gwahrai who raised Oinkillune are a peaceful community, they are no strangers to violence. They constantly live under the threat of not only attacks from the predatory beasts that regularly try to feed on them, but also from the occasional invasion from rogue states. Like most of the members of his adopted race, Oinkillune carries a gwahrai thornstick and sling with him at all times, and is always ready to join his community in defending the village should such an emergency arise. However, despite bearing the responsibility of defending his community, Oinkillune always doubted if he could ever bring himself to use his weapons for lethal force if he needed to. He is someone who shuns violence, preferring to reason with his enemies, and only uses violence when absolutely necessary.

> Until recently, Oinkillune viewed all races of people as being generally good, despite having their small share of evil members. However, something happened to his community that would forever change the way he felt about the human race. A few years ago, the Stevens Empire began expanding their army. Because the interdimensional portal had recently been shut down by Nnet Olotor's good armies, the empire needed vast quantities of building materials to maintain and expand their army. One of these materials was wood, and in order to obtain the amount they needed, the empire began to clear cut the forest the gwahrai depended on for survival. When the gwahrai realized the empire's clear cutting campaign was headed towards their villages, they attacked the empire, driving them away from the forest. However, the Stevens Empire soon returned with a vengeance. Several hundred imperial troops stormed the forest, killing everything in sight, and laying torch to the trees in an effort to burn the gwahrai from their hiding places. With the help of their elven allies, and perhaps even a bit of divine intervention, the gwahrai were able to defeat the invaders, but the price of victory was high. Nearly half of the gwahrai homes were destroyed, and several hundred gwahrai and elves perished, including some of Oinkillune's closest friends. As a result of the attack, Oinkillune developed a deep hatred towards the human race, not just the Stevens Empire, and only the few good humans he has met on occasion prevents him from thinking that the entire race is inherently and totally evil.

> Oinkillune decided to learn as much as he could about the Stevens Empire, and like most Nnet Olotorians, he keeps track of the war between the empire and Nnet Olotor. However, he never bothered to learn too much about Earthen culture and traditions outside of those of the empire. From the time Oinkillune first heard about the war, he had assumed that the evil ways of the Stevens Empire was the norm for the entire human race. During his travels, Oinkillune avoids human cities whenever he can, since he assumes that such places are hotbeds of violent crime. Since his contact with humans is very limited, he has never bothered to learn their languages. Still, when Oinkillune deals with unfamiliar but seemingly friendly Elven speaking humans, he tries to put his prejudices aside and let his true, courteous nature shine through.

> In the years following the attack against his adopted people, Oinkillune developed a strong hatred towards bullies and people who use their power (political power, size, etc.) to forcefully bend others to their will. He never backs down to a bully, nor does he allow anyone to talk down to him, but his hatred often clouds his judgment so that he forgets when he's overmatched. Other than this desire to preserve his pride, he does not suffer any other lapses in his judgment when it comes to violent confrontations, and respects his physical limitations. Even though his contempt for violence has not wavered, he won't hesitate to use it if need be. However, Oinkillune still doubts that he could ever use lethal force if he had to, and never carries his thornstick with the thorn attached.

> At a very early age, perhaps when he was around 12 years old, Oinkillune discovered that he bore magic energy. He was told that since he had never attempted to earn any deity's magic energy, the presence of such energy within him could only mean that he was an angel. Because he was born around the same time as the birth of the Zodian angels, it was highly likely that he was one of those angels. Despite the widespread rumor that Zodian angels are inherently evil, the gwahrai community decided to judge Oinkillune by his good nature, and did not treat him as a pariah. In order to help conceal his identity, Oinkillune's family suggested to him that he should pass himself off as a mage who earned a good deity's magic energy. Although he was able to easily pass as a mage, he saw Zod's energy as a curse and a disease, and always had nightmares about it consuming his soul, and turning him into a monster that would commit unimaginable acts of evil. He had always refrained from using his magic energy in any way until the destruction of his village at the hands of the Stevens Empire. It was then when he decided to take his fighting skills to the next level. Training under experienced fighting mages, Oinkillune began using his magic energy to enhance his fighting prowess, and learned a few magic techniques along the way. Because he was an angel, Oinkillune possessed a tremendous amount of magic energy, and had fighting potential far surpassing that of a normal elf. Despite standing just over five feet in height and weighing a mere 105 pounds, he had the power to single handily bring down a small group of mid-sized humans within the first few years of beginning his training. Realizing the power he possessed, Oinkillune used it to dedicate his life to fighting evil and protecting the innocent, and to hopefully contribute in the destruction of the Stevens Empire. He vowed never to learn or cast any of the actual spells in the magic order he was bound to, since it would mean supporting the interests of the evil deity who created him. Oinkillune hopes to someday find a way to purge Zod's magic energy from his body, and to earn the magic energy of a deity whose ideals match his own.
