Evil Blood: The Zodian Threat
(4/28/2001 - 8/20/2002)

> Nearly 100 years ago, the evil deity Zod was defeated after centuries of war against the good forces of Nnet Olotor. However, before the deity was completely destroyed, he somehow managed to impregnate 10 (or maybe many more) elfin women. According to legend, it may have been possible to revive Zod from his eternal sleep in the body of someone who shared his blood. In order to complete this resurrection, a living descendant of Zod would need to be captured, and forced to be the center in some kind of resurrection ceremony, where the evil deity would be reborn within and take over the descendant's body as his own, allowing his terror to begin anew. Anyone wishing to bring back Zod in this manner would have only one shot at success, as the ceremony would have to occur on the 100th anniversary of his death. And there were, in fact, many Zodian fanatics who looked forward to the day when they could see their god again. However, most people were not thrilled at this possible turn of events. The people who were responsible for Zod's demise swore to prevent the evil deity from rising again, whatever the cost. They began to hunt down and kill anyone who was suspected of being a descendant of Zod. It's one of those things where, over time, good becomes evil and evil becomes good. The very people who saved the world from Zod's genocide were now committing genocide themselves, and the victims were innocent people who's only crime was sharing Zod's blood.
