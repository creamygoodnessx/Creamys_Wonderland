Gwahrai

--- PHYSICAL DESCRIPTION ---

> The gwahrai are a small, tree-dwelling race found almost exclusively in one particular large forest somewhere near Nnet Olotor's equator. They are named by the elves after their battle cry / distress call, which sounds like a high-pitched "gwaaahhhh!". They are naturally equipped for the forest they call home. The avarage adult gwahrai stands about 90 centimeters tall, and weighs around 20 pounds. They have small and slender lemur-like bodies, small heads, long bushy tails, an extra pair of hands in place of feet. They usually stand on all-fours, but can stand on their hind legs for short periods of time. On the ground, the gwahrai get around by bounding and crawling. Because of their lightweight frame and powerful legs, they are able to leap great distances from a standing start, and often use this ability to travel from tree to tree with great ease. Each of the gwahrai's four hands have three very long fingers plus an opposable thumb, so that their hands resemble an Earthen bird's claw, minus the sharp points. The tip of each finger is a sticky circular pad, much like those on the feet of Earthen frogs, allowing the gwahrai to hang onto the sides of trees. They can also hang on branches using their tails. The gwahrai are primarily herbavoires, living on the wide variety of vegitation their forest provides. They also feast on insects when they get a chance. They have an extremely high metabolism, and consume up to half their weight of food daily. Most of the food the gwahrai eat is very high in nutrition, contributing both to their highly energetic nature, and high level of intelligence.

--- PERSONALITY ---

> The gwahrai can be described as a spartan race of people. They focus their lives around raising their young, contributing to their community, and surviving the next predator attack. The driving force of their society is maintaining the prosperity of the community, rather than the acquisition of wealth. Most gwahrai don't understand the concept of materialism, and look down on people who spend so much of their hard earned resources to obtain expensive luxuries. The gwahrai tend to persue only what they need to survive, although they have been known to keep useless items of sentimental value, such as a first tooth, or an item won from a defeated powerful enemy. They prefer practicality over glamour, and would rather wear a comfortable but plain outfit, rather than a beautiful outfit that restricts movement.

--- LANGUAGE ---

> The gwahrais' evolutionary ancestors posessed a very loud distress call which was similar to that of an Earthen sea gull. The language they use today is based on that call, and is spoken through the use of  pitch and volume changes in the call, but also through the limited use of sounds in human and elven lauages, as well as high and low clicks. They are not physically capable of making all of the verbal sounds of a human or elf, and are limited to the use of  'm', 'ow', 'g', 'oo', and 'ah'. To most outsiders, the gwahrai language may sound like primitave howling, while in truth, it is just as intricate and articulate as a language of any other race of people. The gwahrai have no written language. Because of the nature of their language, it cannot be translated into a written form in any known language, simply beacuse no letters exist for some of the gwahrai language's sounds, although the same doesn't apply to the actual meaning of the words.

--- COMBAT ---

> Because the gwahrai share their forest home with predators that constantly hunt them, they have been forced to develop certain defensive methods. They fight in small groups, and do not like fighting out in the open. Since they are almost always significantly smaller than their enemies, they obviously can't win by brute force. Instead, they use their environment to their advantage, attacking the enemy with blowguns and slings while remaining hidden.The gwahrai like to attack in this manner until they have driven off or killed the enemy, or until discovered. If the latter occurrs, the gwahrai then pounce on the enemy in relatively large numbers, and attack with thornsticks and foriegn weapons they obtained through trade. The prefered weapon of the gwahrai is the gwahrai thornstick. The thornstick is a 40 centimeter long club made of the flexible, yet amazingly durable, wood from their native trees. At the end of the thornstick is a razor-sharp thorn, which adds an additional 15 to 25 centimeters to the club. The gwahrai also use small weapons manufactured by members of other races. These weapons are most commonly daggers, but also include short swords (a long sword to a gwahrai), and spike-knuckles. A fighting gwahrai depends on his/her quickness and agility to win a battle, and shuns anything that compromises it, such as heavy armor, and heavy or any two-handed weapon.

> There are no specially trained or professional soldiers. Rather than having a society that depends on an army or police force to protect them, everyone, from the lowest to highest members of society, and of both genders, is trained in the art of combat from an early age. In the event of a predator attack or an invasion, the civilians can postpone their daily duties and go into their "combat mode" at the drop of a hat, in order to come to the defense of their villiage. When fighting along side elves, the contrasting fighting techniques of the elves and gwahrai complement each other well, making them a very formidable fighting force. Fighting gwahrai rely on their great quickness and agility to bring down enemies, and shuns anything that interferes with these qualities. When forced to fight in close quarters, they try to always be ready to dodge their enemies attacks, and immediately launch a counter-attack. Since the gwahrai are used to moving so quickly and fluidly through the trees, swinging on vines, bouncing off and climbing of the sides of trees, and using both pairs of hands and their tails to grab and hang from branches and vines, heavy armor and any two-handed objects would seriously hinder their movements, thus making them far less formidable in battle.
