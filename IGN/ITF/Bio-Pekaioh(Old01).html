Pekaioh
(10/2/2005 to 2/18/2006)

 - CHARACTER STATS -
Real Name - Roonaii Pekaioh
Race - Elven
Residence - ???
Demeanor - Arrogant, Narcissistic, Jealous
Age - Elven equivalent of late teens or early twenties
Height/Weight - 6' 1" / 190lbs
Alignment - Neutral Evil

> The first Zodian War ended several thousand years ago when a super-powerful hero defeated the evil deity Zod in single combat. I haven't yet worked out any of the details of this war, nor any details about the hero, but hopefully that will be taken care of as I continue to develop Evil Blood's background story. Because of the hero's tremendous power and bravery, his spirit was preserved after he died. From that point on, whenever Nnet Olotor was in peril, the spirit of the hero would be reborn within one of his male decendants. There would always be some way for the right people to identify the reborn hero, be it a birthmark, an instictual action, or something else that was unique to the hero. After the new hero was discovered, he would be required to begin training to face the peril immediately, often abandoning his familiar life in the process. The hero would always have access to a particular magic order from birth, and the potential to become an exceptionally powerful mage, much like an angel. But unlike most angels, the hero would be able to access other magic orders as well. The hero's inborn magic order will most likely be the same one as the original hero.

> At an early age, a young elven boy named Roonaii Pekaioh was told that he was the reincarnated spirit of the hero that defeated Zod. After his identity was discovered, he was immediately dragged away from his familiar surroundings, and trained by some of the best clerics and champion fighters to face not only the threat of Zod should he be resurrected, but also growing threat of the Stevens Empire. After a few years of rigorous combat training, Pekaioh became a very powerful fighting mage for someone in his mere adolescence. However, as his magic energy and fighting skills increased, so did his vanity. He began to become more concerned with his public image than his actual level of skill, and wasted a lot of his money on fancy clothes and jewelry, flaunting his image as a legendary hero at every opportunity. He looks down upon less gifted fighters as inferiors, and is very open about his disrespect towards them. Pekaioh thinks of himself as a defender of justice to be worshipped by the masses, and a symbol of extreme fighting prowess to be feared by the forces of evil.

> I'm not yet entirely certain how Pekiaoh will enter the story, but I will most likely have him be an on-and-off member of Jonny-V's party at some point. He may first appear in the story while answering one of Jonny-V's violent public combat challenges. If such a battle were to take place, it would be a very close contest, but Jonny-V would come out on top and spare Pekaioh's life for putting up a sufficiently good fight. Much later on in the series, the two would meet again, but this time Pekaioh would assist Jonny-V and Oinkillune in overcoming the Stevens Empire.
