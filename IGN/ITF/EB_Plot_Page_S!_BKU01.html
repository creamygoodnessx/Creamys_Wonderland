Evil Blood: The Zodian Threat
(Spoiler)

> Zod never completely ran himself out of magic energy, and therefore, never really died. Instead, he was forced into a mortal shell which bore the likeness of whatever his worshippers believed him to look like. When he cast the spell that created his angels, he saved the last bit of magic energy to keep him alive when he retreated into his physical body. Since Zod is one of Nnet Olotor's evil deities, he gains magic energy whenever his worshippers pay homage to him by committing acts of evil, which usually take the form of the sacrifice of a person. Normally, Zod can only gain magic energy when his followers commit such acts. However, when he was trapped in his physical body, he could gain magic energy from any violent death on Nnet Olotor, regardless of who was responsible for any such death. Despite this tremendous advantage to his interests, Zod only used took a bodily form as a last resort for two important reasons. First, while in his physical body, Zod would no longer be able to keep a constant eye on his worshippers (an ability that many other deities have), thus limiting his communicative abilities to person to person contact. Second and more importantly, as a mortal, Zod would be subject to every means of death as any mortal creature, and if he was killed while in his physical body, he would be permanently destroyed along with his magic order. In order to return to full strength, Zod would need tens of thousands of violent deaths, and once this is achieved, he would only need the blood of one of his angels to return to his deity form, giving him another chance to wreak havoc on Nnet Olotor. Although violent deaths on Nnet Oloter give Zod strength, they are not necessary for him to ultimately return to his deity form. Zod could opt to abandon his physical body before he reaches full strength, but it would mean having to get his magic energy back solely by rebuilding his religious community, wasting the violent deaths as part of his plan.

> Besides his lack of magic power, Zod also faced a different problem. During the war against Nnet Olotor's good armies, a great majority of his followers had been killed. Because Zod, like any other deity, relies on the number and loyalty of his worshippers not only to maintain the power of his magic order, but also to stay alive while he's in his deity form, he would need to reestablish his religious community before his return to his deity form. If Zod does not have a considerable base of followers by the time of his return to his deity form, he will gradually lose magic energy after his return, until the amount of his energy once again matches the energy the number and loyalty of his worshippers can provide. I'll have to come up with a way for his followers to start some kind of recruiting campaign to attract new worshippers. I'm not sure how to do this, but the current situation with the Al Quiada may give me a few pointers.

> Because 90 percent of Zod's army had been wiped out, he obviously would have a hard time converting new worshippers, let alone having them carry out the tens of thousands of deaths he needed to return to full strength. However, Zod had anticipated this setback long before his defeat, and since then had come up with a countermeasure. Right after his defeat, he had his followers start a false rumor throughout Nnet Olotor's civilizations that any one of his angels were inherently evil, and could resurrect the evil deity if they chose to do so, and only they knew how to do it. It was Zod's hope that this rumor would trigger a public witch hunting campaign in which enough people would be killed to satisfy Zod's kill quota. The public is not aware of the existence of Zod's physical body, the advantages it gives him, or even the fact that he's still alive. If they were, they would not hunt down Zod's children, but would rather go after Zod himself. So needless to say, the existence of Zod's physical body is a secret that Zod only shares with his most trusted worshippers.

> However, things didn't go exactly according to Zod's plan. Most of the elves were appalled at the idea of killing their own children, even though they 'knew' what the alternative was. Instead, they searched for ways to purge the evil they were led to believe was lurking within their children. However, to Zod's good fortune, one Nnet Olotorian faction was not so compassionate, and felt they needed to do whatever it took to stop Zod from once again defiling their world. So they began to hunt down the children of Zod themselves, managing to slaughter around 300 of their targets, along with the people trying to defend them. The genocidal faction was eventually stopped, and as punishment for their actions, was banished from the mainland.

> Although the deity of knowledge could read the thoughts and secrets of everyone on Nnet Olotor, he could not read those of other deities. This made it possible for Zod to keep his secrets safe from the god of knowledge, including whatever plans he had for his return to power. However, Zod would eventually have to tell at least a few of his followers about his secret if they were to carry out his plans, risking discovery. Long before he ever had to worry about being overthrown, he had his followers build a fortress in an uninhabited and isolated part of the world, and had them install magic inhibitors throughout the building. These inhibitors were designed to block out the magic energy of all deities except Zod, so that only his magic order could exist within the building. Because the god of knowledge's mind reading ability required any recipients to be in contact with his own magic energy, he could not pick up the thoughts of anyone within the boundaries of Zod's fortress, making it the ideal place for Zodians to hold secret meetings. Needless to say, Zod never told anyone about his most classified information until the completion of the fortress. Zod did not worry about the god of knowledge discovering the truth about his plans for his return to the deity form. The false rumors Zod was spreading throughout Nnet Olotor in order to incite its population to hunt down and kill his angels also fooled the deity of knowledge into believing the rumors. This is because the deity of knowledge's only information about the mortal world was what he read from people's thoughts, and the only people who knew the truth were confined within the walls of Zod's fortress. Also, any campaign to destroy the Orb of Knowledge would be in vain, because its deity can always create a new one in its place.
