QuakeCon 2010 - Day One

> I arrived at Philadelphia International Airport sometime after 8AM on Thursday. Because I still didn't trust the TSA fascist pigs to go anywhere near my computer CES, I once again had it shipped down to Dallas through FedEx, while carrying my 17" BenQ monitor with me on the plane. Unlike my last QuakeCon trip, there were no incidents at the airport this time. However, after I boarded the plane to Dallas just before 9AM, the flight was delayed for over an hour due to the weather, causing me to arrive at Dallas Fort Worth around 12:30PM Dallas time instead of the scheduled 11:40PM. Once there, I took a Super Shuttle bus to the Hilton Anatole and got there around 1:15PM. My first goal was to pick up CES from the Business Center, as I did in 2008. However, I had entered the hotel in a place I didn't recognize, and soon realized that I was lost. So I went to a nearby help desk to ask where the Business Center was, and after navigating my way through the unfamiliar part of the hotel, passing by an area which was under construction, I soon reached my destination and retrieved CES's box. The box contained not only CES, but my illuminated Modware keyboard, Razer Copperhead mouse, Flexiglow mousepad that glowed green, GE headphones, and most of my clothing. The box, along with my monitor, was too heavy for me to carry all the way to Trinity Hall where the BYOC event was being held, so I asked for a dolly. In order to ensure the eventual return of the dolly, the person behind the desk held on to my non driver's ID, and soon I was on my way to the BYOC room, pushing the dolly with CES and all of its components on it.

> After getting to Trinity Hall and waiting in a long ass registration line, I had to register for the QuakeCon and give out my personal details on one of their laptops, because the event's organizers had decided to drop the online registration for that year. I was then given my con badge which had a barcode on it, and then had to have a staff member register my equipment for the event. This meant that I had to open the boxes that contained CES and the monitor, but because I left my Swiss Army Knife at home to keep it away from the TSA nazis, I had to use my house keys, which was a real pain in the ass. Fortunately, another nearby staff member happened to be carrying a box cutter which he lent to me, and soon I was able to get both boxes, plus CES's inner box opened. The first staff member then put stickers with barcodes on CES, my monitor, mouse, keyboard, and headphones, and then I wheeled CES over to yet another staff member who scanned the four barcodes plus my con badge.

> After registering, I rolled CES and its components into the BYOC room and looked for a place to set up. I was looking for a table near the aisle, so that passersby could check out my machine, and to increase my chances of having it appear on some other attendees' YouTube videos. In 2008, I had set up right across the aisle from the NOC, but that apparently wasn't enough to get on YouTube, so I was hoping to have better luck this time around. But as I was dragging the dolly behind me in search for a table, CES's inner box tipped over and fell off the dolly, causing CES and some of its peripherals to tumble out of the box and land on the floor. Horrified, I scrambled to get everything back into the box, and some nearby attendees were nice enough to help me. I found a table right next to one of the aisles in row B63. However, the power strips on that table didn't appear to have power, so I moved to the first table in row B65. Sometime after 3:30PM, I had hooked up CES, not knowing if it was going to run at all after the spill it took. Not knowing what to expect, I nervously pushed CES's power button in an attempt to turn the system on, and mercifully, it booted up Ubuntu normally. A little bit later, the monitor's colors started acting weird, and I was afraid that it may have been due to damage to CES from its spill. But since there was nothing I could have done about it, I knew that I would have to live with it until after I got back home.

> Just after 6PM, I tested my Internet connection by going to YouTube and watching one of Thunderf00t's videos. All the web pages I visited loaded very slowly, YouTube's videos were choppy, and I wasn't getting any sound. Disappointed with the slow connection, I decided to play some MUGEN, and while the game itself ran OK, I still wasn't getting any sound. Puzzled, I opened Rhythmbox and tried playing an mp3 of Quad Machine by Sonic Mayhem, but there was still no sound. Once again, I was worried that I had a problem which was the result of damage caused by CES's spill. Not sure if the problem was because of that, or from faulty headphones, I borrowed the headphones from another attendee, and was able to hear music just fine. This suggested that my headphones were indeed the problem, and I knew that I would need to somehow get a new pair.

> Just before 6:30PM, two people from the TastySpleen community recognized me while I was at my table. One of them said hello, and that a lot of the members of the community had already shown up. While checking out the QuakeCon page at home, I saw that TastySpleen was hosting a Quake 2 Tournament at this year's QuakeCon, and I asked the Tastypleen member if that was true. She said that it was, and that I should talk to someone at the tournament registration table if I wanted to sign up. She then gave me a card with a picture of the grand prize, which was a wireframe sculpture of the Quake 2 symbol. I mentioned that I was having a problem getting my mouse to work with Quake 2 in Linux, and she said that Jehar used Linux intensively, and that I should talk to him about it. I walked towards the bathrooms and saw a table with a sign that read "BYOC Competitions", so I went up and asked the people there about the TastySpleen tourney. They suggested that I visit The QuakeCon Tournament Center page at qcc.at.quakecon.org and look up information about the tournament there, so I did. However, I did not see any tournaments mentioning anything about Quake 2 or TastySpleen. Figuring that the Tastyspleen people hadn't put the info of their tournament on their page yet, I decided to forget about it for the time being and move on to other matters.

> While doing some further browsing, I came across a 3D shooter that looked very similar to the game XIII. The game turned out to be the creation of someone who was creating his own 3D shooter that ran on an engine that he created himself. It was called Last Bastion, and was still under heavy development, since there were still untextured models and unfinished animations, and the game only had one level completed so far. The programmer let me try it out myself, so I did, and I was very pleased with its progress. At 6:30PM, I packed up all the empty containers and loaded them onto the dolly, then wheeled everything all the way back to the Business Center in the main building, returning the cart and getting back my non-driver's ID. I then immediately went to the front desk and checked into my room, and the desk clerk gave me two keycards and told me that my room was 4160. When I got there with my empty boxes in hand, I settled down, took a shower, and then returned to the BYOC room sometime before 8:30PM, leaving the boxes in my room. While rechecking the tournament page, I happened to peek down and caught a glimpse of the cord of my headphones. When I did, I saw that the headphones had a volume dial, something that I had completely forgotten about. After doing the obligatory facepalm, I realized that I no longer needed to buy another set of headphones, and that was a huge burden off my shoulders.

> Just before 9PM, I saw a guy with a Tastyspleen T-shirt walk by while I was playing MUGEN, so I asked him about the status of the Quake 2 tournament. He went by the name Square, and he said that someone called Railwolf was in charge of the tournament, and that he would let him know about my query. Around 9:45PM, I ordered a pepperoni pizza and a 2 liter bottle of Coke from Zinis Pizzeria, and was told that my food would reach the hotel in about 30 to 45 minutes. While I was waiting for my order, I went back to the BYOC room and checked out some console games that some attendees brought with them, including Mario Kart for the Wii, Super Smash Brothers, and Street Fighter 4. Half an hour after calling Zini's, I went to the lobby to wait for the pizza guy, and he was right there when I walked in. After paying for my order and tipping the pizza guy two dollars, I went upstairs to get some ice, poured some Coke in my purple drinking bottle, and brought the pizza, purple bottle, and 2 liter Coke bottle back to down CES. I then spent the rest of the night sitting at CES, playing some more MUGEN, and some Stepmania as well.
