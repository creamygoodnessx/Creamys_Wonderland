QuakeCon 2010 - Day Three

> After disappearing for a while, Square came back with the Memory Stick in hand just after midnight, and said that the Quake 2 source code was on it. I put the stick into my Connectland card reader, and copied a folder named APRQ2 to my home folder. Square then took over, going to the copied folder in the command line, and entering "make" to compile the code. Unfortunately, he got an error and realized he needed to compile some dependencies, something that would have taken a long time, so he left the table and said he would return soon. Just before 1AM, I ran to the Information Desk to ask what the word was on the case mod contest. The staff there said that the judges would be in the main building sometime after sunrise, and that it would be announced over the loudspeaker in the BYOC room when they were ready to begin the final round. Soon after that, Square showed up again and said that his team had been sidetracked, and that he would try to help me compile APRQ2 after sunrise, but suggested that I try to do it myself in the meantime. Sometime after 1:30AM, three people dressed as ninjas showed up at my table, playing some kind of weird music. One of the ninjas had and electric guitar, and another had an electronic oscillator which he used as a musical instrument. After 2AM, I finished work on Brandy's last Stance sprite (Stance08) for her swim trunks outfit, and left the BYOC room around 4PM to get to bed.

> Sometime after ten that Saturday morning, I got out of bed, showered, changed into my QuakeCon 2008 shirt, and went back to the BYOC room. I immediately went to the Info Desk, and again asked about the case mod contest, and the staff said that they were meeting in the grand ballroom in the main building at 1PM. Just before 11:30AM, I called up Dominos and ordered a small pizza, some Cinnisticks, and a 2 liter bottle of Coke which were supposed to get to the hotel in about 30 minutes, and then headed back to CES. On the way back, I talked to someone else who had also entered his machine in the case mod contest, and he was nice enough to call one of the judges on his cell phone and ask him about the contest. After the call, he told me that judges did their rounds between 8PM and 11PM the previous night, which was when my flag went missing. Twenty minutes after I ordered my pizza, I got a call from the hotel's front desk on my cell phone saying that my pizza had just been delivered, so I hurried over and picked up my order and left a tip of over $2.50.

> An announcement came over loudspeaker at noon, saying that all BYOC tournaments were now open and that all participants should go over to the BYOC Competitions table and officially register by filling out a form. As for the TastySpleen Quake 2 tournament, CES still wasn't ready to run the game, so I reluctantly went to the qcc.at.quakecon.org website and withdrew from the tournament. A short time later, another announcement came over the loudspeaker, this time saying that the registration for some Street Fighter tournaments were starting, so I rushed over to sign up for the Street Fighter 3: Third Strike tourney. After waiting in line, I talked to the people at the registration desk, and they said that they were taking registrations for Street Fighter 4 and Super Smash Brothers only, and that the registration for SF3 would be announced later.

> I returned to my table, and soon after I did, PeeWee from the TastySpleen community showed up and offered to help me get Quake 2 running on CES. I ran the game and showed him my problems with getting the mouse to work, and the problem I was having with compiling APRQ2. The tourney was about to start, and it looked like there was no hope of me joining, but then another member of TastySpleen by the name of Amp offered to let me borrow his machine so I could compete. So I hurried over to his machine and rushed back to qcc.at.quakecon.org to re-enter the TastySpleen tourney, but as I was doing so, it was announced at 1PM over the loudspeaker that the finals of the case mod contest was starting in the main ballroom, so I rushed over there as fast as I could. When I got there, a staff member said that if I wasn't told that I made it into the final round, then I didn't make it. Knowing the case mod contest was over for me, I hurried back to the TastySpleen tables to rejoin their tournament. I was teamed up with vi0cs, and we played two rounds against Quest and Gator, and we got our asses handed to us both times, which eliminated me from the tournament. I had been recording my two rounds as I played, and after the rounds were over, I copied the recorded games onto my Connectland USB hub / card reader, and went back to CES around 3PM. As a final note on the TastySpleen tourney, Quest from the community came by at around 4:15PM and gave me a TastySpleen T-shirt just for competing.

> I spent the next several hours playing DJ Hero alongside another attendee on his machine, trying unsuccessfully to get Totem to play my Simpsons Movie DVD, and playing MUGEN amongst other things. I also kept checking the console section of the BYOC room to see if the registration for Street Fighter 3 was taking place, and each time, I was told that it hadn't yet. However, when I checked back at 10:30PM, it turned out that I had missed out on the registration. Fortunately, one of the staff members was kind enough to give me his slot, and I spent the rest of the night waiting patiently in the console section for my turn.
