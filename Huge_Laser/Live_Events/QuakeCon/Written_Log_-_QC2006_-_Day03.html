QuakeCon 2006 - Day Three

> At around 12:30AM on Saturday the 5th, there was yet another round of T-shirt throwing, this time from the nVidia stage. After walking to various places in the crowd trying to find a good spot to catch a shirt, I found myself on the right side of the stage (facing towards the stage), about six feet away from it. The guy on stage saw me and threw a shirt directly at my chest, and I managed to catch it unopposed. I went back to my table with my new QuakeCon 2006 T-shirt, and put it in my backpack for safe keeping. I spent the next few hours doing various things, including watching some more Guitar Hero for another hour or so, and then went back to my room, where I went to bed sometime before 5:30AM.

> After getting a few more hours of sleep, I went back to the BYOC room, and went to the nVidia stage to enter CES in a case mod contest which started a little after 10AM. The catch was that this was an ugliest case mod contest, but I thought that CES would have a chance because of its crude look, mostly in the front panel which was made entirely of junk parts. The woman who was hosting the contest called up the contestants one at a time and asked us to describe our machines any why we thought they were ugly. I was called up first, and I basicly said that CES's ugliness came from its crude look, but some people in the crowd thought it was pretty good looking. The other entries included a machine with no power switch that had to be turned on and off by bridging the power switch pins with a metal instrument, an extremely dusty box, and a machine that was covered with decals and belonged to the contestant's daughter. After the descriptions were over, four finalists were chosen, and not surprisingly, I was not one of them. The host announced the names of each of the four finalists in turn, and the crowd would cheer according to how ugly they thought the finalist's case was. The winner was Phat_Jesus, and his prize was the first pick between three brand new computer cases, while second (Zebaca) and third (thatGUY) picked from the other two. There was also an ugliest mouse and ugliest keyboard contest, but since I was totally unprepared for either, I brought CES back to my table and hooked it back up.

> Just before 1PM, I answered a request made from the Noc to volunteer some time and give something back to the event. I found myself sitting at the check out table close to the exit door, where I spent the next four hours checking the boxes and bags of people exiting the BYOC room, and comparing their items to their registration sheet. One of the people who were checking out was SK|tox, a Swedish gamer who was supposed to be one of the best Quake 4 players around. When my shift ended around 5PM, I met Frankie at the Activision section, and he said he would meet me in the neighboring room at 6PM. A little later on, I got to show Frankie CES, and he seemed to like my case mod and asked about where I got the front panel, what type of motherboard it used, etc.

> Sometime before 6PM, I was on CES when all of a sudden I heard several people moan in disappointment all at once. At first, I had no idea what they were upset about, but I soon realised that the power had gone out in my part of the room. The only thing that kept me from noticing the power outage right away was the fact that CES was hooked up to my fully charged UPS, and this made hauling around the extra 40 pounds worthwhile. The people at the Noc suggested that we unplug our machines in order to avoid drawing too much power from the generators at once. I was afraid that there would be a power surge when the power was restored, so I shut down CES and unplugged and turned off my UPS just to be on the safe side, and the last thing I needed to do was risk frying CES's third motherboard.

> With nothing to do on CES for a while, I went to the neighboring room around 6PM where I met a few members of the Polycount community, including Frankie, Bronco, and Mr. Rockstar. We took some group photos, and so I lent someone my Cybershot to take a couple shots with it. Unfortunately, the camera's battery was very low, and the camera shut itself off before the guy could take the shot. However, several cameras were being used, and so a few pictures did manage to get taken, including this one. As I was heading back to the BYOC room to see if power to my section had yet been restored, I heard a crowd cheering in suspense at something. When I took a closer look, I saw that two people were stacking plastic cups really high. My camera couldn't stay on for more than a few seconds, but I was lucky enough to get off this crude shot of the stacking.

> When I got back to the BYOC room, I saw that the Directron people were dismantling their booth, and nearly sold out all of their merchandise. One of the few things they had left in stock were black xRaider mousepads that had clear borders which were illuminated by LEDs. I wanted to buy a green pad, but the only color pad the booth had left were red ones. So I bought a red-lighted mousepad for $15, and figured that I could replace the red LEDs with green ones after the trip. The best part about the purchase is that I didn't have to pay a shipping handling charge, and I didn't have to wait for it to arrive at my house. However, a few days later, sometime after I got home from my vacation, I was disappointed to learn that I could have bought a green LED version of the same pad at Directron.com for less than ten bucks.

> Around 6:45, the Noc people announced that power in our section would be restored in less that 15 minutes, so I turned the UPS and CES back on without plugging the UPS back in. This way, CES would run off of the UPS's battery, and the UPS's 50 minutes of power would be more than enough to wait out the rest of the blackout. Even though I was able to use my computer, I couldn't access the Internet, and this was probably because the QuakeCon's router was offline. When the power was still out 20 minutes later, I got a little worried that my UPS would run out of juice before my section's power came back, so I decided that if the power didn't come back in 20 minutes, I would shut down my computer. But it never came to that, because my section's power was restored half an hour after the announcement, so all I had to do was plug the UPS back into the outlet. However, I still had to restart CES to re-establish my Internet connection.

> Sometime around 10PM, I saw a small group of people gathered around a computer three rows in front of me and making lots of noise. I couldn't see what they were looking at, because their screen was facing away from me. When I went over for a closer look, I saw that they were watching lesbian porn, and this was distracting the guy playing Quake 3 on the next computer to the right, causing him to frequently get killed. Soon, there was a contest going on in which people would take turns playing Quake 3 with lesbian porn playing on the neighboring computer while wearing the headphones that played the porn's audio for an added distraction. After watching that bizzare contest for a few minutes, I played a bit of Street Fighter Zero 3 on Game Not Over, and then spent the last couple hours of the night on CES.
