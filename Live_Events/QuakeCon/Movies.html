Creamy's QuakeCon Movies

QuakeCon 2006 - This is the first QuakeCon in which I took movies of my experiences. I used my Sony Cybershot DSC-P92 digital camera, which doesn't perform well in low light, and can't zoom in or out in movie mode.

QuakeCon 2008 - Once again, all movies I shot of the QuakeCon were done with my Sony Cybershot DSC-P92 digital camera, which is still serving me well despite showing its age on occasion.

QuakeCon 2010 - The Sony Cybershot DSC-P92 digital camera I used for the previous two events was starting to unexpectantly stop while recording movies. So this year, I brought along a new Cybershot DSC-W330 instead. This model of the Cybershot performs better in low light than the DSC-P92, but the audio input sucks.

QuakeCon 2012 - Just like the previous QuakeCon I attended, I brought along my Cybershot DSC-W330 to record movies of the event. Unfortunately, the camera was beggining to show its age, and I had to discard some really good footage for being severely out of focus. The overall quality of the movies taken at this year's event is much higher than those taken in 2010 due to being shot at a higher resolution, but the files are a lot bigger as a result.

QuakeCon 2014 - Yet again, I used my Cybershot DSC-W330 to record the movies for this video log. The focusing problems I had in 2012 continued to get worse, and this became very apparent in the movies I shot later on in the event.
