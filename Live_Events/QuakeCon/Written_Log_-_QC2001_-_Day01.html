QuakeCon 2001 - Day One

> My plane arrived at DFW about half an hour earlier than expected. The previous night, I had received my new IBM_ThinkPad, and spent all night installing software on it. I was also stuck with packing for the trip, and preparing for the head modeling contest that never came to be, so I ended up getting no sleep at all that night. I couldn't get any sleep on the plane either due to lack of comfort, but somehow I wasn't tired when my parents and I got to the QuakeCon. When standing in line for the registration for the BYOC, I met HarlequiN for a brief moment, and had for lunch a 2 dollar slice of pizza. When I finally got into the event, I was greeted with the sight of literally hundreds of machines, some of them with homemade chassis. (My favorites being the one with the round window with the green neon rim, and the black one with the blue christmas tree lights.)

> I set up my ThinkPad, which I'd named Glamdring, on the seventh table from the rear wall near the Men's room. I was frustrated by the fact that Quake 3 Arena was running really slowly, and by the jumpiness of my mouse. I also had lost the sheet that told me the IP settings I needed to connect to the BYOC network, so I had to call my parents from their hotel room and ask me to send me back my passport, so I could get another IP address. When I finally got myself online with a bit of help from the QuakeCon staff, I downloaded WinZip and the latest version of Q3A, the latter seemed to solve both of the problems I was having with the game. But then, just as I thought my worries were over, the QuakeCon's generator overheated, resulting in a blackout. The unmistakable sound of the power-down filled the room as the machines all around me shut down. But there were survivors. The first seven rows in the front of the room were unaffected, while everything else suffered. I was in the back of the room, but because of my fully powered Li-Ion battery, Glamdring remained operational, while those around me fell. And if one blackout wasn't enough, we had three more before the day was through.

> That night, I met Polycount guys on balcony by the pool. This had to be the highlight of the day; meeting the people of the community that I'd been a part of for the past two years in person for the first time. As far as I remember, I met Phuky, TNSLB, rogue13, Wrath, Goatman, HarlequiN, and bearkub. As far as my dinner went, my parents were kind enough to stop by with some chicken and fries, but in my excitement over the Q3A match I was in, I only managed to finish about three quarters of my food. Before the night was through, I talked to the Generations Arena mod team, and was amazed by the amount of work they put into their mod. They mentioned an early attempt at their mod being stalled by legal issues, but I can't remember the details. Goatman and I returned to our room (211) at the Comfort Inn late that night, maybe around 2 or 3 in the morning, where Goatman gave me a sneak preview of his Big Guy and Rusty Quake 3 model.
