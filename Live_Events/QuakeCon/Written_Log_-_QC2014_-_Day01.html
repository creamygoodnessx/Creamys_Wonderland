QuakeCon 2014 - Day One

> Early Thursday morning on July the 17th, my plane touched down at the Dallas Fort Worth airport in Dallas, Texas, and soon afterwards just before 10AM, I boarded a Super Shuttle bus and headed for the Hilton Anatole hotel. I arrived at the hotel around 10:15AM, and went straight to the Business Center to pick up the two big boxes containing my computer, The Creamy Entertainment System, and its ViewSonic 17" monitor. After paying a $40 fee and signing up for the boxes, the FedEx people there gave me the boxes which were already on a dolly. I asked them if I could borrow the dolly so I could take the boxes to the BYOC room, and they said that I could, but I would have to leave something behind as collateral in order to ensure that I brought the dolly back. So I left my backpack behind, and then wheeled the cart towards Trinity Hall where the BYOC was taking place. In the hallway between the hotel's main building and Trinity Hall, a QuakeCon volunteer placed barcoded stickers on both CES and the monitor, and gave me a waiver to fill out and sign. I then moved on to Trinity Hall itself, and when I got there, I found that the line was very short, and was confined to the room just before the BYOC room. After filling out the waiver, a couple more volunteers asked for it, along with my ID and registration info. This turned out to be a problem, because the sheet that had my registration info was in my backpack back at the Business Center, but I was able to use my Samsung Galaxy smartphone to access my Hotmail e-mail account, where I opened up the e-mail page with the registration info I needed. The volunteers scanned the barcode on the page and the barcode stickers on CES and the monitor, and I was soon on my way to the BYOC room.

> Once I entered the BYOC room, I searched for my reserved table, but I was unable to find it on my own, so I went over to the NOC and asked someone there to help me out, and after giving her my handle (Creamy Goodness), she told me that my table was B34-16, and I then I was able to find it with minutes. Once there, I dropped off the two big boxes at the table just after 11:30AM, and then wheeled the dolly back to the Business Center where I retrieved my backpack. I then took my bag back to the BYOC room, but a volunteer at the room's entrance examined my bag and found my Toshiba laptop Gwizdo, and said that I had to have it registered in order to have it stay in the room. But when I went back to the registration room, to have it registered, the volunteers there said that I couldn't do that, because I could only register one computer for the event. So I checked into my hotel room, which turned out to be 514 in the Atrium, brought my backpack there, took out Gwizdo and its components, and then returned to the BYOC room with a much lighter bag in hand. When I got back to my table, I brought CES and its components out of their boxes and hooked everything up, but when I turned on the machine, all the pretty lights turned on, but nothing appeared on the screen. To cut a long story short, I had to remove the green LED mods from the two RAM sticks, reseat the RAM sticks several times, have the RAM sticks switch places, and then put the RAM mods back on. After tinkering with the machine for over an hour, and almost running down the battery on my Samsung Galaxy smartphone by using its Droidlight app, I finally got CES up and running around 3:50PM.

> After I got CES running, I played a few rounds of Quake 2 on the Tastyspleen servers, and then ordered some pizza, Cinnasticks, and a two liter bottle of Coke from Dominos, which all arrived at the Anatole's Tower lobby less than a half hour later. I took the food upstairs to my hotel room and had some of the pizza and Coke while watching Samurai Jack on Gwizdo. I returned to the BYOC room just before 9PM, and spent the rest of the night playing the Armageddon wad for Doom 2, and then a little bit of Hedgewars.
