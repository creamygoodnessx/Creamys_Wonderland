QuakeCon 2012 - Day Four

> By the time midnight arrived, I had filled up all the space on my 8GB Memory Stick due to all the video footage I shot of Parker and his wearable rig, so I took this time to copy the data onto both CES and my 32GB Patriot flash drive, and purge the Memory Stick's data. After playing some Super Punch Out on ZSNES, I left the BYOC room just before 2AM to get some sleep in my hotel room. I got out of bed at 7AM, took a shower, and packed up all of my belongings from my hotel room, double checking to make sure I didn't miss anything, and then left Room 574 for the last time. From the room, I carried my suitcase back to CES in the BYOC room, where I ate the last slice of pizza that I got from the Pizza Hut truck the previous day with some Coke.

> At 9AM, I shut down CES and started to dismantle my setup, and it took me an hour to pack everything into their boxes and my backpack. With everything packed up, I went to the bell stand and called for a bellhop to bring a cart to the BYOC room, had all of my equipment checked out of the BYOC, brought both boxes and my suitcase to the Business Center on the cart, then tipped the bellhop seven dollars. I then resealed the two boxes, and the FedEx guy at the Business Center put CES's box into another slightly bigger box with paper filling. Since CES's RAM modules had come loose on the way to Dallas, I wanted to avoid that happening again on the return trip, which is why I asked for the second box. With an already prepared shipping label now taped to CES's outer box, the two boxes were ready to be shipped back to New Jersey, and so I left the boxes at the Business Center taking only my backpack and suitcase, checked out of the Hilton Anatole just before 11PM, and got on a Super Shuttle bus to Dallas Fort Worth about ten minutes later.

> This year's trip had its share of high points as well as low points, just like any QuakeCon trip I've taken so far. Other than the long-ass line on the first day, and the fact that I once again couldn't get Quake 2 to run, there were no serious downsides to this year's trip, and I ended up having a good time. I met some really cool people, showed off CES, and saw some interesting case mods. I also picked up a lot of cool stuff, including a Notepal X3 laptop cooler for $30, a Doom T-shirt for $18, four cans of Bawls I bought from other attendees, a free QuakeCon T-shirt, and a free QuakeCon plastic cup. The boxes containing CES and its monitor arrived at my house the Thursday after the QuakeCon, and this time CES's memory modules had not shaken loose, and the system started up without any problems. This may or may not be my last trip to the event, depending on a number of factors, including whether or not I can get anyone to go with me during my next trip in 2014, and whether or not I can get Quake 2 running on CES prior to that year's event. My nephew Austin will be 14 years old in 2014, and I'll see if I can get him to tag along with me to that year's QuakeCon.
