QuakeCon 2012 - Day Two

> I went to bed the next day around 2:45AM, got out of bed around 10AM and took a shower. For the next few hours, I played some SuperTuxKart on CES, and caught up on my QuakeCon log. Around 5:45PM, I dismantled CES's setup, packed CES and its monitor into their boxes, packed the rest of the components into the appropriate boxes or my backpack, and painstakingly carried everything back to the Pre-Function Area of the Trinity building. Some of the attendees had set up their computers near to the wall outlets in the room, and I thought about whether or not I should do the same. I ended up deciding against it, since I didn't know what was going to happen next. So I went up to the registration desks and told the volunteers there that I had a voucher for the BYOC, and asked what line I should get into. The volunteers said that they would let everyone know what to do soon, so I got into the same place in line where I was last night.

> Around 6:30PM, the line for the attendees with the vouchers was started on the left side of the room, and I joined it as fast as I could. Throughout the previous day, I kept hearing people say that the BYOC $30 Select-A-Seat reservations would expire at 7PM on the current night, and become open to the First Come First Serve attendees. However, that didn't happen when 7PM actually came, and the announcement of the seats becoming available didn't come until twenty minutes later. Even then, there was no sign of anyone in any of the lines getting into the BYOC until 8PM when another announcement was made; the BYOC attendees who were camping out all night the previous night and throughout the current day would get priority, followed by those with vouchers, and then everybody else. Soon afterwards, we were given name badges that we had to write our handles on, and one page waivers we had to fill out. The people in the voucher line didn't even have to go to a table to get registered, because the volunteers had portable scanners and were scanning the barcodes of our name badges, registration confirmation sheets, and the equipment tags on our computers and monitors. When we got into the BYOC room, we had to go to a table to have our bags and boxes checked for contraband, and then to another table to be assigned seats. Just before 8:30PM, I reached my workspace, which was very small, but just big enough to accommodate CES and all of its components.

> Sometime after 9PM, an announcement came from the Information Desk saying that the Pizza Hut truck outside was about to leave, and was selling large pies for only $5, so I hurried over to the truck, and so did about fifty other people. Some of them cut through the BYOC line and one of the volunteers yelled at them, asking them to go around the line. The line to the pizza truck was long but moved quickly, and when I got to the front, I tried to order a medium pepperoni pie, but they were out of them, so I settled for a small personal pie for two bucks and a 20 ounce bottle of Pepsi for another two bucks as my meal for Friday. The rest of the night was relatively uneventful, as I basically spent it playing SuperTuxKart, playing Tetris 2 and Super Punch Out on the ZSNES emulator, and watching Phineas and Ferb on YouTube.
