QuakeCon 2014 - Day Four

> After I finished my pizza, I flossed and brushed my teeth while watching more playback matches of Quake 2, and then went to bed around 1:30AM. After sunrise, I called the Super Shuttle bus service to arrange for a bus to come by and take me to Dallas Fort Worth at 11:30AM that morning, and then left the room to return to CES around 9:45AM. When I got back to the BYOC room, almost everyone had already left, and only the last few hardcore attendees remained. I picked up a black garbage bag from a janitor's cart, and went back to CES to catch up on my QuakeCon log. At 9:30AM, I shut down CES and went back to my room, taking the garbage bag with me. I stuffed all of my dirty laundry into the bag, dismantled Gwizdo's workstation, packed all of my non-laundry belongings into my backpack, and left 514 for the last time.

> With my backpack on my back and the garbage bag in my hand, I returned to the BYOC room, but the volunteer checking my bag said that I couldn't take Gwizdo into the room with me, but she offered to hold onto it until I was ready to leave the room, I took Gwizdo and its mousepad out of the bag and put it aside. When I got back to CES, I dismantled the workstation, packed everything into their two boxes, and left the room to pick up a wheeled dolly, leaving the two boxes behind. I went to the Business Center to borrow their dolly again, but the FedEx people there said that they didn't lend out their dollys, and suggested that I go to the bell stand instead, so I did. When I got there and asked to borrow a dolly, David, the person there, said that I would have to wait ten to fifteen minutes for the dolly, and he would call my name when it was ready to go. This concerned me, because it was already after 11AM, and my Super Shuttle bus was scheduled to pick me up at 11:30AM. When the dolly was finally ready about 10 minutes later, another hotel employee named Robert wheeled the dolly to the BYOC room, and I led him to my table in row B34. I had forgotten to pack my now half-full bottle of Coke that I had ordered from Dominos into my boxes, and I couldn't take it on the plane with me because the TSA Nazis wouldn't allow it, so I gave it away to the attendee sitting in front of me. After Robert and I put the two boxes onto the dolly, we headed for the BYOC room's check out line near the exit, but instead of checking my boxes, the volunteers let me exit the room without opening them, because the teardown was less than an hour away, and they no longer had time to check everyone's equipment. I retrieved Gwizdo and its mousepad from the volunteers who were holding onto it for me, and then Robert and I headed out.

> After leaving the BYOC room for the last time, I followed Robert to the Business Center while he pushed the dolly, and when we got there, he took the boxes off of the dolly and I tipped him four dollars. I then handed a shipping label to the FedEx people that my mother had filled out before the trip, and then waited until they finished preparing the boxes for shipping. It was then when I noticed that the clock in the Business Center read exactly 11:30, and I realized that I would probably miss my ride to the airport. After a few minutes I put Gwizdo and its mousepad back into my bag, and soon after that, the FedEx people handed me a receipt and said that I was good to go. I then went directly to the hotel's front desk where I checked out of my room, but it was already around 11:45AM, and when I rushed outside to see if the Super Shuttle bus was there, it was nowhere to be found. So I went back to the Bell Stand for help, and David said that the Super Shuttle busses came by the hotel every half hour near the Atrium's exit, and the next bus was due to arrive in about five minutes. With no time to waste, I rushed over to the Atrium's exit, but the noon bus never showed up. At this point I began to panic, because my plane back to Philadelphia was scheduled to depart at 1:45PM, and now there was a real possibility that I could miss my flight. However, the 12:30PM bus actually did show up, and I hopped on board the front passenger seat. Soon, I was finally on my way to Dallas Fort Worth, and when I got there, I tipped the driver five dollars. With only minutes until the 1:15PM boarding time, I got my boarding pass at the kiosk, went through security without incident, and went to gate A33. Luckily, the plane was about ten minutes late to start boarding, so that ensured that I would catch my flight and be on my way home.
