QuakeCon 2014 - Day Three

> After finishing my pizza, I flossed and brushed my teeth, and then went to bed sometime after 12:30AM. I got out of bed after 8PM, showered, put the clothes I wore on Friday back on, and then returned to the BYOC room where I played a little more Stronghold. After a while, the cold of the BYOC room was getting very uncomfortable for me, so just before 10:30AM, I went back to the vendor's room to see if there were any clothes I could buy that would warm me up. At the Glitchgear_booth, there was an assortment of clothes, so I asked one of the vendors if he had a hoodie, and said that he did, and he showed me some of the hoodies he had in stock. There were a few Assassin's Creed hoodies, but there was also one with the Aperture Laboratories logo from the Half-Life series. The hoodie was a small size, but I normally wear large size shirts, so I tried it on to see if it would fit. The fit was OK, so I gave the vendor $40 and walked back to CES with my new Aperture Laboratories hoodie. I then pulled my Doom_T-shirt and a pair of jeans from CES's two boxes, and brought them up to my hotel room so I could change into them in privacy. With my new clothes on, I returned to Trinity Hall, where I asked people at the BYOC registration booth if I could get the free QuakeCon 2014 T-shirt that was included in my $50 Select-A-Seat package. The volunteer at the booth directed me to a booth in the vendor's room where I could pick it up, so I went to that booth, and when I did, the guy there scanned my con badge and gave me a large QuakeCon 2014 T-shirt.

> Sometime around 1:15PM, the guy sitting to my right told me that the people at the Alienware vendor's booth in the BYOC room were giving away free T-shirts, so I went to the booth and picked up an extra large T-shirt. Just after 5PM, the Modders-Inc judges came by my table in order to examine CES. One of the judges remembered my machine from when I entered in 2012. I showed the judges two new features I added to CES since the last tournament, which were a new pair of RAM mods, and a cold cathode grill, both of which were controlled with a set of three new switches on the front of the machine. When the judges left to go to the next contestant, they told me that they would talk to all of the contestants by 6PM and tell them who was moving on to the finals and who wasn't. After the judges left, I called up my nephew Austin and asked him if he wanted to join me in a Quake 2 deathmatch on the Tastyspleen servers. He agreed to my invitation, and he used the copy of Quake 2 I copied to his flash drive several months earlier to join me on Tastyspleen's Vanilla server. After a few games, Austin took a break so he could install Quake 2 on a hard drive of one of his computers, and this allowed me, Austin, and Austin's brother Brandon to all play on a DM server. But after a few games, Austin said that he had to take another break to have dinner, and asked me to call him back in about an hour.

> I stayed at my table non-stop from the time the Modders-Inc judges left until well after 7PM, and the judges never returned as promised, so I assumed that I once again failed to make it to the finals and gave up on the case mod contest altogether. Just after 7:40PM, I called back Austin, and the two of us played on the DM server again until 10:15PM, and the two of us stayed on the phone the entire time. In fact, my headphones weren't even plugged into CES during the gaming session, but instead were plugged into my Samsung Galaxy smartphone so I could talk to Austin. Although I couldn't hear CES's audio output, I could hear the game on the phone through Austin's speakers in the background. By the time our two hour plus gaming session was over, my left ear was hurting because I was wearing the headphones for so long, and now I was forced to take them off and massage my ear. I left the BYOC room before 11:30PM and went back to the outdoor food court, and bought a 20 ounce Coke and a pepperoni pizza from the Yummy Pizza truck, then took my meal up to my hotel room where I spent the rest of the night enjoying it while watching playbacks of my Tastyspleen Quake 2 deathmatches on Gwizdo that I played earlier that day with Austin.
