QuakeCon 2010 - Day Four

> Just after midnight, the staff member at the console game section of the BYOC approached me where I was sitting at the console gaming section and told me that my turn to play in the Street Fighter 3: Third Strike tournament had come. I ended up playing two games against a much better player, and we both picked Ken for both matches. Not surprisingly, I lost straight rounds in both matches, although I didn't get my ass kicked as badly as I thought, as I never lost a round perfect, and ended up draining my opponent's health halfway in one round. Satisfied with my chance to participate in the tournament, I returned to my table.

> I left the BYOC room to return to 4160 to get some sleep at 4AM. After getting out of bed at 9:45AM, I took all of my belongings out of room, knowing that I would never be returning. I brought the empty boxes back down to the BYOC room, and used them to pack up CES and all of its components. The unopened box for the Cyborg Keyboard was too large to fit in CES's outer box, so I decided to ship it home separately. Once everything was packed up and in either in a box or my backpack, I went to the bell stand for a dolly, and a bellhop by the name of Tadesse followed me to the BYOC room where I had packed of CES, pushing the dolly in front of him. I put CES's box, the monitor box, the Elite Keyboard box, and my backpack onto dolly, had my equipment checked out of the room, and then the two of us went to the Business Center where I took everything off the dolly and tipped Tadesse five dollars. I then resealed CES's inner and outer boxes and the monitor box with tape, and left CES's box and the Elite Keyboard in the Business Center, taking only my backpack and monitor box. Just after noon, I checked out of the Hilton Anatole, then called up FedEx to schedule a pickup for the two boxes I left in the Business Center.

> Just before 1PM, I took a look at the printout of my airline schedule, and saw that the plane I was supposed to take home was scheduled to take off at 12:55PM, meaning that I would never get to Dallas Fort Worth on time. So I called home to explain the situation to my mother, and she rebooked my flight for 4PM, but she later called back and said that the airline had made a mistake, and the flight was atually taking off at 5:50PM. With my return flight sorted out, I took the 1:30 Super Shuttle bus to the airport and got the around 2PM. As luck would have it, the flight was delayed due to nearby lightning strikes, so just like the trip to Dallas, I once again had wait in the plane for over an hour before taking off.

> My fifth trip to the QuakeCon had its ups and downs, just like all of my previous trips to the event, and was well worth the thousand dollars I paid for the plane ticket, hotel room, and other expenses. Although I had my share of problems, mainly the tournament related mishaps, and the QuakeCon's shitty on and off Internet connection, I had plenty of good things happen that more than made up for them. I got a high end keyboard at a significant discount, drew two sprites for my Brandy Oteigh_MUGEN character, met the TastySpleen people in person, and had about a dozen people stop by and admire CES's beauty. CES falling off of its dolly on the first day of the event gave me quite a scare, and although I knew that the machine still had its audio capabilities, I was not so sure about the video, because the colors were acting weird throughout the event. But after I got home and plugged CES into a different monitor, I saw that the colors were just fine, indicating that the monitor was faulty and that CES had not been damaged when it was dropped. Finally, as the result of the help I got from TastySpleen, I got Quake 2 to run normally on CES less than a week after the event. Thanks for reading, and I hope to be able to go back to the QuakeCon in 2012.
