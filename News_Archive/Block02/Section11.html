News Archive - Section 11

8/11/2009 - I have a very important announcement to make, and this will significantly affect the entire site. This is going to be the very last site update for Creamy's Wonderland as a part of the IGN network, because IGN is dropping its hosted site feature at the end of this month. However, there is good news.  Many of the websites hosted at IGN, including this one, are moving to a new host by the name of Huge Laser. This announcement, by the way, was not the one I mentioned during the last update, and since that one pales in comparison to the host change, I'll just say it now. The Evil Blood section is shutting down permanently, since I have lost interest in the story and haven't been able to come up with any new ideas lately. The next time I update the site, that entire section will be history.

> As for the actual site updates, I've got more of the usual news coming out of the Games / MUGEN / Characters section. My edit of Kenshin Himura's doujinshi Bubbles has been updated to Version 1.01_CGX03 with two new changes. I've also finished editing Hiram Yagami's Ryoko Izumo with over 50 changes, the most I've done for any character edit so far. As for Brandy, I have replaced her walk animations with new ones, and used the old ones for her alternate state, the Stone Aspect. Also Brandy's two existing counters have been upgraded to be easier to use, making her slightly more competitive. In order to illustrate these changes, I've included three new movie clips on her page. And finally, the losers' quarter-finals round A of the Double Jeopardy 2008 tournament is now available for your viewing pleasure.

5/17/2009 - It's been nearly half a year since my last update, and I don't really have all that much to show for it besides a few updates in the Games / MUGEN section. Under Characters, I'm now hosting Kamek's Reiko Hikari and Byakko's Kim Kaphwan. Brandy Oteigh has six new animations, including four throws, two of which are special attacks. I also have the first field test video of Brandy's gameplay on her character page. And last but not least, in the AI Tournaments section, the winners' quarter-finals round of the Double Jeopardy 2008 tournament is up and running.

4/19/2008 - The Games / MUGEN section now includes its first two sub-sections; Characters and AI Tournaments. Characters will include my original characters, both in their finished and work in progress stages. Right now, the only original character in the section is a WIP called Brandy Oteigh, which I hope to finish in the upcoming months. Also included in Characters is works from other artists that I've edited, as well as really good characters that were taken offline by their authors for whatever reason. AI Tournaments will feature fights between the AI of characters in a special roster I've set aside for this purpose, and will be updated with a new tourney round on every site update. Also, In Games / Stepmania, I did some minor updates, mostly cosmetic, to several stepfiles in CreamyMix 1.

12/9/2007 - Just thought I'd squeeze in one last site update for the year, and this time it's a big one. I've recently finished the third quarter of CreamyMix 1, bringing the total number of songs in the mix to fifteen. There is a new page in the Art Gallery called Fan Art, and it includes three pics I drew of characters from some of my favorite animated TV shows. I'm also starting a new MUGEN section under Games, which will feature various AI tournaments between some of the characters in my roster, as well as characters I plan on creating. Finally, I've made a small update to the Links page.

> As for the Builds section, there is no update to CES's build log this time around. In fact, I'm not going to update that section again for a while. Before I touch the build log again, I'm going to downsample and crop the rest of the build photos and add them to the photo log, and then write and store all of the written log pages locally. This way, when I'm ready to update the build log, I can simply post an already finished written log page on the site so I don't have to scramble to write it at the last minute.

In the Games / Stepmania section, I renamed some version numbers of some of CreamyMix 1's songs to more accurately portray the difference between major and minor changes to the stepfiles. In the Builds section, I posted Section 16 of the written log of the Creamy Entertainment System. The Games / Quake 2 section also got a minor update; I renamed the hand grenade skin of John McClane's VWEP so that the grenade model links correctly to it. And in case you didn't notice, I finally posted a new front page mural called Let's Dance after three years of having Splash Area on the page up here. Let's Dance is the first mural on Creamy's Wonderland to show the site's new, shorter URL.
